# Engine Design

The Growth Engine is the system that builds on your foundation to create sustainable, compounding growth. It's powered by three interconnected motions: Acquisition, Monetization, and Retention. Channels are tactics — motions are the structure those tactics fit into.

---

## The Three Motions

### Acquisition Motions (how you bring users in)
| Motion | Time-to-Ignite | Velocity | Intent/Context | Cost |
|--------|---------------|----------|---------------|------|
| **Virality-led** | Slow-Med (needs critical mass) | Very High | Low | Very Low |
| **Paid-led** | Fast (scalable instantly) | High | Low-Med | Low |
| **Content-led** | Slow-Med (library + distribution build) | Medium | Med-High | Medium |
| **Sales-led** | Slow (requires reps + pipeline) | Low (human bandwidth) | High | High |

### Monetization Motions (how you convert users to revenue)
| Motion | Time-to-Ignite | Velocity | Intent/Context | Cost |
|--------|---------------|----------|---------------|------|
| **Product-led (self-serve)** | Fast | High | Low-Med | Very Low |
| **Hybrid** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Low-Med |
| **Sales-led** | Slow (constrained by hiring) | Low | High | High |

### Retention Motions (how you keep users engaged)
| Motion | Time-to-Ignite | Velocity | Intent/Context | Cost |
|--------|---------------|----------|---------------|------|
| **Product-led** | Medium | Med-High | Low-Med | Very Low |
| **Hybrid** | Fast-Med | Medium | Medium | Low-Med |
| **Sales-led** | Slow | Low | High | High |

## How the Motions Connect (Flywheels)

When aligned, the three motions create a compounding cycle:

### 1. New Customer Flywheel (Acquisition + Monetization)
Fuel → New user acquired → User converted to revenue → Revenue reinvested as more fuel

### 2. Engagement Flywheel (Retention)
User engages → Receives value → Engagement deepens → Receives even more value

### 3. Revenue Expansion Flywheel (Retention + Monetization)
User retained → More revenue → Revenue reinvested → More retention

**Monetization is the bridge** — it converts value into fuel and channels it back into acquisition or retention.

### Conditions for a Powerful Flywheel
- **Positive feedback loop**: Each cycle produces more output than input
- **High velocity**: Short time between input and output
- **Low leakage**: Outputs can be reinvested (not lost to poor retention or inefficient monetization)
- **High ceiling**: Room to keep compounding (paid/virality need huge markets)

---

## Motion Alignment (4 Factors)

Not all motion combinations work well together. Evaluate alignment across:

### 1. Time to Ignite
How long to reach full velocity potential. Fast acquisition + slow monetization = bottleneck.

### 2. Velocity
How quickly users move through the motion once established. High-velocity acquisition needs high-velocity monetization.

### 3. Commitment
The level of effort users must give. Key rule: **low-commitment entry → low-commitment next step.**
- Virality/Paid = push mechanism → needs light asks (free trial, frictionless onboarding)
- Content = opt-in, heavier investment → users primed for higher-commitment asks
- Sales = layers context over touches → gradually raises commitment

### 4. Cost (CAC Spectrum)
Your motion combination determines your CAC band:

| Acquisition | Monetization | Typical CAC |
|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Virality-led | Product-led | Lowest ("True PLG") |
| Content-led | Product-led | Low |
| Paid-led | Product-led | Medium |
| Any channel-led | Hybrid | Medium-High |
| Sales-led | Hybrid | High |
| Any channel-led | Sales-led | High ("Inbound Sales") |
| Sales-led | Sales-led | Highest ("True Sales-led") |

**Your ARPU must sustain whichever CAC band you choose.**

## B2B vs B2C Differences

**B2C typical patterns:**
- Paid-led or Virality-led acquisition + Product-led monetization + Product-led retention
- Emphasis on speed, self-serve, and low CAC

**B2B typical patterns:**
- Content-led or Sales-led acquisition + Hybrid or Sales-led monetization + Hybrid retention
- Higher CAC tolerable due to higher ARPU
- Sales involvement increases with deal size

## Questions Sequence

1. Which acquisition motion fits your product? (Paid/Content/Viral/Sales) Why?
2. Which monetization motion? (Product-led/Hybrid/Sales-led) Why?
3. Which retention motion? (Product-led/Hybrid/Sales-led) Why?
4. How do these three motions align on Time-to-Ignite?
5. How do they align on Velocity?
6. How do they align on Commitment levels?
7. What CAC band does your combination create? Does your ARPU sustain it?
8. Where's the flywheel potential? (New customer, engagement, or revenue expansion?)
9. What's the biggest alignment risk in your current combination?
10. What secondary motions might you add later?
