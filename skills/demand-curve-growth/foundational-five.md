# Foundational Five Assessment

The Foundational Five (F5) framework assesses and defines four foundations — Market, Product, Brand, Model — then checks alignment between them before defining the fifth: Channel. This is Phase 1 of the growth system.

## How Scoring Works

Each foundation has input fields. Count your **high-confidence answers** (backed by research or data, not speculation). Use the traffic light thresholds to determine readiness.

---

## Part 1: Market (13 inputs)

Ask these questions to define the Market foundation:

1. **Market Category** - The broad category your market falls into (e.g., college students, B2B marketers, healthcare professionals)
2. **Specific Segment** - The targeted segment within that category (e.g., pre-med students, SEO marketers at agencies)
3. **Total Potential Customer Base** - How many potential customers are in your specific segment
4. **Willingness to Pay (WTP)** - What customers are willing and able to pay for solutions like yours
5. **Buyer vs User** - Whether the person who pays is the same as the person who uses the product
6. **Core Problem** - The primary problem your market faces (bonus: in the customer's actual words)
7. **Motivations** - Why they want to solve this problem — the underlying drivers
8. **Values & Beliefs** - What your market believes in and values most deeply
9. **Where Do They Spend Time** - Online and offline, in the context of their problem and in general
10. **Who Do They Trust** - Influencers, publications, peers, or authorities they listen to
11. **Problem Aware? Solution Aware?** - Are they actively seeking solutions, or do you need to educate them?
12. **Market Shifts / Macro Trends** - External forces affecting behavior (cultural trends, regulatory shifts)
13. **Competitors & Alternatives (+ satisfaction)** - Who they currently use and how satisfied they are

**Scoring:** Count high-confidence answers:
- **< 7 = Red**: Complete Market Research and Competitive Research deep-dives, then return
- **7-10 = Yellow**: Proceed to Product
- **11+ = Green**: Proceed to Product

---

## Part 2: Product (12 inputs)

1. **Product "One-Liner"** - What your product is, in simplest terms (e.g., "Finance app for kids")
2. **Job-to-be-Done** - Complete: "When ___, I want to ___, so I can ___."
3. **Problems It Solves** - The specific problems your product addresses
4. **Outcomes / Value Created** - The results customers get from using your product
5. **Core Capabilities ("How It Works")** - Key functionality and mechanisms
6. **Differentiator** - What makes you meaningfully different from alternatives
7. **Ease of Explanation** - How simple or complex is your product to understand (from market's POV)
8. **Time to First Value (TTFV)** - How quickly users get their first meaningful result (minutes/days/weeks/months)
9. **Natural Usage Frequency** - How often users engage (daily/weekly/monthly)
10. **Inherently Social?** - Does it naturally encourage sharing? (collaborative tools, social apps)
11. **Content/Artifacts Created via Usage?** - Does using it create outputs that have value?
12. **More Value with More Users?** - Do additional users make it more valuable for existing users?

**Scoring:** Count high-confidence answers:
- **< 6 = Red**: Proceed (Story System module will deepen product inputs later)
- **6-9 = Yellow**: Proceed to Brand
- **10+ = Green**: Proceed to Brand

---

## Part 3: Brand (7 inputs)

1. **Brand Innovation** - How central brand innovation is to your strategy
2. **Brand Story** - The narrative about who you are, what you stand for, and why you exist
3. **Brand Personality** - The human characteristics and traits your brand embodies
4. **Brand Risks & Guardrails** - What you must avoid to maintain brand integrity and trust
5. **Primary Promise** - The main commitment you make to customers
6. **Proof of Promise** - Evidence that you can deliver on your primary promise
7. **Context of Encounter** - Where and how customers typically interact with your brand

**Scoring:** Count high-confidence answers:
- **< 4 = Red**: Proceed (Story System module will deepen brand inputs later)
- **4-5 = Yellow**: Proceed to Model
- **5+ = Green**: Proceed to Model

---

## Part 4: Model (7 inputs)

1. **Model Innovation** - How central model innovation is to your strategy
2. **What Do You Charge For?** - The specific value, features, or outcomes customers pay for (e.g., time subscribed, product access, transactions)
3. **How Do You Charge?** - Pricing structure (monthly subscription, one-time, % of transaction, flat CPM)
4. **How Much Do You Charge?** - Specific price points and tiers (e.g., "7-day free trial then $20/mo")
5. **Gross Margin (%)** - (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue * 100
6. **Expansion Lever(s)** - How average revenue per user increases over time (upgrades, add-ons, increased usage)
7. **Pricing Policies** - Discounts, guarantees, risk-reduction offers (parity pricing, scholarships, money-back guarantees)

**Scoring:** Count high-confidence answers:
- **< 4 = Red**: Monetization & Pricing deep-dive recommended
- **4-5 = Yellow**: Proceed to Alignment Check
- **5+ = Green**: Proceed to Alignment Check

---

## Part 5: Cross-Fit Alignment Check

After defining all four foundations, assess how well they work together across 5 pairings. For each pairing, answer 4 assessment questions.

### Pairing 1: Market <-> Product
Key alignment questions to consider:
- Specific Segment <-> Job-to-be-Done: Is the JTBD truly aligned with your segment?
- Core Problem <-> Product Solution: Does the product solve the core problem in the market's words?
- Motivations <-> Outcomes/Value Created: Does the product produce outcomes enabling core motivations?
- Core Problem <-> Natural Usage Frequency: Does expected usage align with realistic behavior?
- Buyer vs User <-> TTFV & Ease of Understanding: Is the product designed for the actual user?

### Pairing 2: Market <-> Brand
- Specific Segment <-> Brand Story: Does the story reflect how the segment sees itself?
- Motivations <-> Brand Personality: Does personality evoke emotions related to core motivation?
- Core Problem <-> Primary Promise: Does the promise mirror the desired outcome?
- External Context <-> Proof of Promise: Are proof assets credible in their context?
- Where They Spend Time <-> Context of Encounter: Is brand touchpoint aligned with where they are?

### Pairing 3: Market <-> Model
- WTP <-> How Much You Charge: Is price aligned with realistic WTP?
- Market Size <-> Price Point: Does price x market size support your financial goal?
- Problem/Solution Aware <-> How You Charge: Is pricing structure aligned with awareness stage?
- Core Problem <-> What You Charge For: Does what you charge for map to core problem?

### Pairing 4: Product <-> Brand
- Brand Personality <-> Product Experience: Does the product feel like the brand personality?
- Brand Story <-> JTBD: Does the product interface express the brand story?
- Primary Promise <-> TTFV: Do features deliver the promise (not just aspirational)?
- Proof of Promise <-> Outcomes: Are proof cues visible where users experience value?
- Brand Guardrails <-> Usage Patterns: Do product prompts honor brand guardrails?

### Pairing 5: Product <-> Model
- Value Prop <-> Price: Does delivered value justify the price?
- Usage Frequency <-> Charge Method: Does natural usage align with how you charge?
- Core Capabilities <-> Gross Margin: Can capabilities sustain target margins?
- Network Effects <-> Expansion Levers: Do usage patterns support revenue growth?
- TTFV <-> Pricing Policies: Do activation patterns support required payback periods?

### For Each Pairing, Ask:
1. **Strongest alignment**: Where do you have the strongest alignment?
2. **Biggest gap**: Where is the biggest gap?
3. **Biggest risk**: If this gap persists, what's the most probable negative consequence?
4. **First adjustment**: What single, concrete change would most directly address this gap?

### Priority Assessment
After all 5 pairings, identify:
- **Highest Priority Risk**
- **Second Priority Risk**
- **Third Priority Risk**
