# Acquisition Strategy

The Acquisition Strategy defines which channels to pursue, in what order, and why. It bridges your engine design (motions) to the tactical execution layer (specific channels).

---

## Channel Evaluation Framework (7 Criteria)

Score each channel on these 7 dimensions:

### 1. Scalability (Reach x Ramp)
- **Reach**: How much of YOUR target market you can actually reach (not the channel's total users)
- **Ramp**: How far/fast you can increase investment before plateau (unit economics breaking, audience saturation)

### 2. Targetability
How precisely you can target your specific audience segment.
- High: Paid ads, Sales (can pick exact segments)
- Medium: SEO (keyword-based), Email
- Low: Virality, Content marketing (little control over who sees it)

### 3. Intent
Whether users' mindset on the channel aligns with buying your product.
- High: Google Search (actively seeking solutions), comparison sites
- Medium: LinkedIn (professional context), Pinterest (shopping-adjacent)
- Low: Facebook/Instagram (social browsing), TikTok (entertainment)

### 4. Context Fit
Whether your brand/product aligns with the channel's culture and usage patterns.
Consider: device (mobile vs desktop), culture (channel norms), relevance (topic alignment)

### 5. Effort
Time and expertise needed. Two dimensions:
- **Setup effort**: Initial investment to get the channel running
- **Maintenance effort**: Ongoing work to keep it performing

### 6. Cost
Financial resources required:
- **Setup cost**: Building the infrastructure
- **Maintenance cost**: Ongoing spend to sustain results

### 7. Time to Results (TTR)
Total time to meaningful results (activation time + time to cross meaningful threshold).
- Fast: Paid ads (days)
- Medium: Sales, founder-led social
- Slow: SEO, content library, virality

## Bullseye Framework

Start close to the bullseye and work outward.

### Bullseye (Inner Ring)
Highest intent + context fit channels. Usually lower scale but highest ROI and fastest payback.
- These are where you learn what messaging resonates
- Build conversion fundamentals here first

### Middle Ring
Good channels that need more sophistication to crack. Medium intent/context.
- Pursue after validating messaging and funnels on bullseye channels

### Outer Ring
High scale but low intent/context. Requires significant expertise.
- Easy to get false negatives testing these too early
- "Channel X doesn't work" usually means "we tested it before we were ready"

**Key principle**: Prioritize context + intent over scale. A smaller, more aligned channel beats a huge misaligned one for early-stage companies.

---

## Demand Capture vs Demand Generation

### Demand Capture
Reaching people who are already looking for a solution.
- Google Search Ads, SEO (bottom-of-funnel), comparison sites, marketplace listings
- Highest intent, lowest effort to convert
- Limited by existing search volume

### Demand Generation
Creating awareness and desire among people who don't know they need you yet.
- Social ads, content marketing, influencer marketing, PR, community
- Larger potential scale but lower intent
- Requires stronger messaging and longer nurture

### Splitting Resources
- Start with demand capture (if volume exists) — fastest path to revenue
- Layer in demand generation as you scale and capture channels plateau
- Balance depends on your market's awareness level (from F5: "Problem aware? Solution aware?")

## Channel Sequencing

### Step 1: Shortlist & Score
- Filter channels to your acquisition motion type
- Score each on the 7 criteria
- Note foundation fit, growth advantages, and guardrail constraints

### Step 2: Pick Flywheel Channel(s)
- Select your #1 flywheel channel (potential to compound and sustain)
- Optionally pick a Flywheel #2 runner-up

### Step 3: Add Secondary/Assist Channels (up to 3)
- **Kickstarters**: Seed the engine until flywheel is viable
- **Boosters/Linears**: Bridge constraints
- **CAC blenders**: Lower blended CAC or shorten payback

### Step 4: Sequence & Prioritize
- Start close to bullseye (high intent/context)
- Map to guardrails (if flywheel isn't viable yet, start with kickstarters)
- Balance demand capture (short-term signal) with demand gen (long-term compounding)
- Consider a **First Action Bridge** if direct-to-purchase is too costly

---

## Testing Framework

### Minimum Viable Test
- Define what "success" looks like before testing (based on Target CAC)
- Set a budget and timeline ceiling
- Run long enough to get meaningful signal (not just clicks, but conversions)

### When to Call a Test
- Consistently exceeds Target CAC after optimization → move on
- Shows promise but needs refinement → iterate before scaling
- Beats Target CAC → scale investment

---

## Questions Sequence

1. What's your demand capture opportunity? (Is there existing search volume for your solution?)
2. What channels align with your acquisition motion type?
3. For each candidate channel: score on 7 criteria (Scale, Target, Intent, Context, Effort, Cost, TTR)
4. Which channels are your bullseye (highest intent + context)?
5. What is your #1 flywheel channel?
6. What 1-3 secondary/assist channels bridge your current constraints?
7. What's your testing sequence (1 → 2 → 3)?
8. What does a successful test look like for each? (metrics, budget, timeline)
9. What's your First Action Bridge if direct-to-purchase is too costly?
