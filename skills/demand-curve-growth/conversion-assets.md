# Conversion Assets

Conversion assets are the tactical outputs where your story system meets the customer. Each asset — landing pages, emails, ads, onboarding — follows proven structures. Creativity in conversion funnels is generally a bad thing. A confused person bounces.

---

## Landing Pages

### The Purchase Rate Formula

> Purchase Rate = (Desire + Confidence) / (Effort + Confusion)

Increase desire and confidence. Decrease effort and confusion.

### Standard High-Converting Structure

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Navbar** | Logo + navigation. Ideally sticky with CTA button. |
| **Hero** | Header, subheader, CTA, image. Determines if they stay or bounce. |
| **Social Proof #1** | Logos of press coverage or well-known customers. |
| **Benefits & Objections** | Key value propositions fully written out. |
| **Social Proof #2** | Testimonials or customer results. |
| **FAQ** | Answer major questions and objections. |
| **CTA** | Dedicated section to drive action. |
| **Footer** | Miscellaneous links. |

Every section should have a CTA. Never leave the visitor without a clear next step.

### Hero Writing Process

**Step 1 — Value prop**: What bad alternative do people resort to without your product? How is your product better? Turn that into an action statement.

**Step 2 — Add a hook** (choose one):
- **Bold claim**: Specific, triggers "wow, I didn't know that was possible"
- **Objection handling**: Address the #1 reason people don't buy

**Header litmus test**: If a visitor reads ONLY the header, will they know exactly what you sell?

**Subheader**: 1-2 sentences. Either explain how the product works, or explain why the bold claim is believable.

### 7 Questions Your Landing Page Must Answer

1. **What is the product?** (Explain it like you would to grandma)
2. **Is it right for me?** (Be clear whose problem it solves)
3. **Is it legit?** (Invest in design, at least 80/20)
4. **Who else is using it?** (Social proof — the more, the better)
5. **How much?** (Don't waste people's time hiding pricing)
6. **Where can I get help?** (Easy + obvious contact)
7. **How do I take action?** (Obvious CTA matched to lead temperature)

### Conversion Quick Wins

- Optimize for all devices
- Put the form in the hero if the next step is subscribe
- Never assume they understand your terms or jargon
- CTAs in every section + sticky navbar CTA
- Use images to aid understanding
- Make it skimmable — people don't read
- There's no such thing as too much social proof
- A/B test if you have traffic; get feedback if you don't

## Email Marketing

### Three Email Types (Priority Order)

#### 1. Transactional Emails (must have now)
Auto-sent when users take actions. Three tiers:
- **Tier 1 Critical**: Welcome/signup, email verification, password reset, payment confirmation, payment failed, trial ending
- **Tier 2 Important**: Trial expired, login notifications, usage limit warnings
- **Tier 3 Nice to have**: Support ticket created, account deletion confirmation

#### 2. Onboarding Emails (should have soon)
Guide new users to the lightbulb moment. Prefer event-triggered over time-based.
- Email 1 (immediately): Welcome + first action
- Email 2 (day 2): Key feature + tutorial
- Email 3 (day 5): Success story + advanced tips

#### 3. Win-back Flow (build at 100+ users/month)
2-3 emails for users who've gone cold:
- Email 1 (Day 7): Gentle check-in — "Everything okay?"
- Email 2 (Day 14): Show value — what others are achieving
- Email 3 (Day 21): Last chance — special offer

#### Triggers:
- Trial users: no login for 7 days
- Free users: no key feature use for 14 days
- Paid users: no login for 30 days

### Email Anatomy

**Subject lines** — Pull from your Story System hooks:
- Curiosity-driven: "The 2-minute rule that changed everything"
- Benefit-focused: "Cut your [pain point] in half"
- Personal/Direct: "Quick question about your [topic]"
- News/Update: "[Feature] is live (and it's pretty cool)"
- FOMO (use sparingly): "24 hours left"

**Body structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 sentences) — lead with main point/benefit
2. Explanation (2-3 sentences) — why this matters to them
3. Proof/Example (optional) — customer result or data
4. Call-to-Action — one clear next step

**Best practices**:
- One CTA per email
- Action-oriented language ("Start your trial" not "Learn more")
- Keep paragraphs 1-3 sentences
- Mobile-friendly (16px+ font)
- Plain text or simple HTML (image-heavy → spam)

## Ad Creatives

### Core Elements

Every ad = text + visuals. Broken into: hook/angle, visuals, CTA, format.

### 10 Ad Hook Types

| Hook Type | Matching Angle |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Native UGC, fast-cutting edits, lo-fi/ugly |
| **Curiosity Gap** | Before/after, hidden benefits |
| **Problem-Agitate** | Direct-response, testimonial-driven |
| **Social Proof** | Expert endorsement, data-driven |
| **Us vs Them** | Competitive comparison, value-based |
| **Contrarian** | Thought leadership, disruptive trends |
| **Persona Callout** | Niche-focused, pain point solving |
| **Authority** | Expert-driven, trust-building |
| **POV** | Relatable storytelling, lifestyle-driven |
| **Listicle** | Quick takeaways, informative & structured |

### 7 Visual Principles

1. **Instantly understandable** — clear in 1 second
2. **Show, don't tell** — show the problem being solved
3. **Mimic native content** — blend into the platform
4. **Clean & uncluttered** — one key message per visual
5. **Use contrast** — bright colors, bold text
6. **Interface hi-jacking** — use familiar UI (text messages, notes, tweets)
7. **Visual hierarchy** — most important thing most visible

### Ad CTA Best Practices

- Direct action words: "Shop Now", "Get Started", "Try It Free"
- Benefit-driven: "Claim Your Free Trial Today"
- One CTA per ad
- Only add CTA if it provides secondary info or pushes to next action

## Onboarding System

### The Lightbulb Moment

The moment users think: "This actually solves my problem" or "I can't believe how easy that was."

**It is NOT**: completing setup, watching a demo, reading features, creating an account.
**It IS**: experiencing genuine value, solving a real problem, getting meaningful results.

#### Discovery Process

**Step 1**: Complete this sentence 5 ways:
"My lightbulb moment is when users _____ and they think/feel _____"

**Step 2**: Validate each hypothesis:
- Can this be achieved in the first session? (ideally 15 min)
- Is this clearly valuable? (they'd tell a friend)
- Can we measure when this happens?
- Does this connect to core product value?

**Step 3**: Choose primary lightbulb moment as north star for onboarding.

### Backwards Design Method

Build the path from lightbulb moment backwards, not from signup forwards.

**Step 1 — Prerequisites Analysis**: List everything required for the lightbulb moment. Categorize as Critical / Helpful / Optional.

**Step 2 — Minimum Viable Path**: Include ONLY critical prerequisites.

**Key test**: "Could they experience the lightbulb moment without this step?" If yes, move it after.

### Elimination Strategies

| Instead of... | Do this... |
|---------------|-----------|
| "Create your project" | Show sample project |
| "Import your data" | Show sample data |
| 15 program options | "Most popular for beginners" |
| Detailed preferences | "We'll customize as you use it" |
| Skill assessment | Try the beginner version |

### Three Types of Friction

| Type | What it is | Fix |
|------|-----------|-----|
| **Cognitive** | Too much thinking required | Smart defaults, progressive disclosure, clear examples |
| **Emotional** | Anxiety, overwhelm, uncertainty | "You can change anytime" language, social proof, preview value |
| **Mechanical** | Too many clicks/fields/time | Social auth, skip email verification, loading indicators |

### 5 Common Friction Patterns

1. **Verification Barrier**: Let them use product first, verify when needed
2. **Perfect Profile Trap**: Name + email only, collect more contextually
3. **Feature Overwhelm**: Show one capability, reveal gradually
4. **Empty State**: Pre-populate with sample data
5. **Configuration Maze**: Smart defaults + "Customize later" link

### Proxy Value Framework (Complex Products)

For products where value takes time (fitness, learning, community):
1. Define ultimate user transformation
2. Create stepping stone moments (proxy progression)
3. Design immediate first-session value (5-minute quick win)

Key principles: make it personal, show quick progress, build streaks, celebrate milestones, preview the future.

## Questions Sequence

### Landing Page
1. Does your header pass the litmus test? (Would they know what you sell from just the header?)
2. Which hook type for the header — bold claim or objection handling?
3. Do you answer all 7 questions?
4. How many social proof elements do you have?
5. Is there a CTA in every section?

### Email
6. Are your Tier 1 transactional emails working?
7. Do you have an onboarding sequence? Is it event-triggered?
8. Do you need a win-back flow? (100+ users/month?)

### Ads
9. Which hook types best match your story system?
10. Do your visuals pass the 1-second clarity test?

### Onboarding
11. What is your lightbulb moment?
12. What's the minimum viable path to get there?
13. What friction can you eliminate today?
14. Do you need proxy moments for delayed-value products?
