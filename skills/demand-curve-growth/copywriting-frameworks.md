# Copywriting Frameworks

Copy is the lowest-cost, highest-leverage growth lever. A headline change can lift conversions 20% in 20 minutes. These frameworks ensure every piece of copy — ads, emails, landing pages, social — follows a proven structure.

---

## Three Fundamental Rules

### Rule 1: Write So They Can Visualize
Bad copy describes. Good copy paints a picture.
- Use concrete, sensory language over abstract claims
- Replace vague benefits with specific scenarios
- Test: Can a reader "see" what you're describing?

### Rule 2: Write So They Can Falsify
If your claim could apply to any competitor, it's too vague.
- Add specifics: numbers, timeframes, mechanisms
- Replace "great customer support" with "average response time: 47 seconds"
- Swap logos test: if a competitor could run this copy unchanged, rewrite it

### Rule 3: Write What's Unique
Don't echo competitors. Say what only you can say.
- Study competitor copy to know what's already claimed
- Find your proof point: data, methodology, integration, cultural stance
- If it's not differentiated, it won't be remembered

## Seven Copywriting Frameworks

### 1. PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solve)
- **Problem**: Name the specific pain
- **Agitate**: Twist the knife — show consequences of inaction
- **Solve**: Present your product as the resolution

Best for: Ads, email subject lines, landing page headers. Works when the pain is well-known.

### 2. BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
- **Before**: Describe the painful current state
- **After**: Paint the aspirational future
- **Bridge**: Your product is the bridge between them

Best for: Email marketing, social posts, testimonial-style copy. Works when transformation is the key message.

### 3. Storytelling (Hook → Tension → Resolution)
- **Hook**: Open with a surprising fact, question, or scene
- **Tension**: Build conflict or stakes
- **Resolution**: Your product resolves the tension

Best for: Long-form content, founder stories, case studies. Works when emotional connection matters.

### 4. AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
- **Attention**: Grab with a bold claim or question
- **Interest**: Provide supporting details and proof
- **Desire**: Make them want the outcome
- **Action**: Clear, specific CTA

Best for: Sales pages, product launches, full-funnel sequences. The classic direct response framework.

### 5. PASTOR (Problem → Amplify → Story → Transformation → Offer → Response)
- **Problem**: Identify the pain
- **Amplify**: Show the cost of the problem persisting
- **Story**: Tell a relatable story (yours or a customer's)
- **Transformation**: Show the before/after
- **Offer**: Present what you're selling
- **Response**: Ask for the action

Best for: Long-form sales letters, webinar scripts, high-ticket offers.

### 6. 4Ps (Promise → Picture → Proof → Push)
- **Promise**: Lead with the key benefit
- **Picture**: Help them visualize having the benefit
- **Proof**: Back it up (testimonials, data, case studies)
- **Push**: Drive to action with urgency or scarcity

Best for: Landing pages, product pages, email campaigns.

### 7. PRUNE (Remove what doesn't work)
Not a writing framework — an editing framework:
- **P**urge: Remove unnecessary words and sentences
- **R**eplace: Swap weak words for stronger ones
- **U**nify: Ensure consistent voice and message
- **N**arrow: Focus on one idea per section
- **E**valuate: Read aloud — does it flow?

Apply PRUNE after drafting with any other framework.

## 10 Hook Types

Hooks are the first thing people see. They determine whether anyone reads the rest.

| # | Hook Type | Structure | Example |
|---|-----------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **Credibility** | Lead with proof or authority | "10,000+ teams switched this year" |
| 2 | **Fear** | Highlight what they'll lose | "Your competitors already figured this out" |
| 3 | **Curiosity** | Open a loop they need to close | "The one metric most startups ignore" |
| 4 | **Counter-Narrative** | Challenge conventional wisdom | "Forget everything you know about SEO" |
| 5 | **Eloquence** | Say something so well it sticks | A memorable, quotable statement |
| 6 | **Faces** | Feature a recognizable person | Founder story, influencer endorsement |
| 7 | **Value** | Lead with the offer/benefit | "Get your first 1,000 users in 30 days" |
| 8 | **Surprise** | Open with something unexpected | A stat, fact, or claim that breaks expectations |
| 9 | **Celebration** | Tap into shared identity/pride | "Built for founders who do things differently" |
| 10 | **Identity** | Speak directly to who they are | "If you're a solo founder wearing 12 hats..." |

### Hook Selection Guide
- **Top of funnel** (awareness): Curiosity, Counter-Narrative, Surprise, Fear
- **Mid funnel** (consideration): Credibility, Value, Faces
- **Bottom funnel** (decision): Credibility, Fear, Value, Celebration
- **Retention** (engagement): Identity, Celebration, Eloquence

## Copy Audit Checklist

Before publishing any piece of copy, verify:

- [ ] Can they visualize it? (Rule 1)
- [ ] Can they falsify it? Is it specific? (Rule 2)
- [ ] Is it unique to you? Would swap-logos fail? (Rule 3)
- [ ] Does it follow a framework structure? (not rambling)
- [ ] Is the hook strong enough to stop scrolling?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA? (confused mind says no)
- [ ] Does the copy match the funnel stage? (awareness ≠ decision copy)
- [ ] Has it been PRUNEd? (read aloud, cut the fat)

---

## Questions Sequence

1. What are the 3 most important messages you need to communicate?
2. For each message: which framework fits best? (PAS for pain-heavy, BAB for transformation, etc.)
3. What specific proof points do you have? (numbers, testimonials, case studies)
4. Write 3-5 hooks using different hook types. Which stops you mid-scroll?
5. What funnel stage is this copy for? Does the hook and CTA match?
6. Apply the 3 rules: Can they visualize? Falsify? Is it unique?
7. PRUNE: Read aloud. What can you cut?
