# Monetization & Pricing Strategy

Your monetization strategy shapes how value flows from your product into revenue. It's bigger than just picking a price — it's about deciding what to charge for, how and when to charge, and how much to charge.

You don't need perfection. You need to land in the right ballpark and align with your Foundational Five.

**Four common mistakes**: pricing too low, charging the wrong way, guessing on monetization, leaving it to the last minute.

**Four principles**: monetization isn't set in stone, customers influence pricing, precision isn't the goal, action beats theorizing.

---

## The Three Monetization Questions

### Question 1: What to Charge For (Value Metric)

The value metric is the proxy for customer value — the unit they pay for.

**Three broad categories:**
- **Feature-based**: Users get more value by accessing additional features
- **Usage-based**: Users get more value by using more of the same feature
- **Outcome-based**: Value maps to a measurable outcome the product produces

**Selection criteria:**
- Clear for customers (they instantly understand what they're paying for)
- Aligned with value scaling (the more value they get, the more they pay)
- Easy to implement in the short term (if stuck between two, default to this)

### Question 2: How & When to Charge (Structure & Timing)

**How (pricing structure):**
- Flat-rate (one price for everything)
- Tiered (good/better/best)
- Per-user/per-seat
- Usage-based (pay for what you use)
- Variable/hybrid

**When (timing):**
- Monthly subscription
- Annual subscription
- Per-transaction
- Free trial → paid
- Freemium → paid
- Upfront payment

### Question 3: How Much to Charge (Price Point)

**Quick WTP Tool** (adapted from Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter):
Ask 3-5 prospects/customers these 4 questions:
1. At what price would this feel **too expensive** to ever consider?
2. At what price would it start to feel expensive, but still worth considering?
3. At what price would this feel like a **good deal**?
4. At what price would it feel **so cheap** you'd question the quality?

Triangulate a reasonable price band from these responses.

## Pricing Research Methods

### Direct Customer Research
- Van Westendorp (4 price questions above)
- Customer interviews about value perception
- Survey willingness-to-pay ranges

### Competitor-Based
- Map competitor pricing side by side
- Identify where you sit relative to alternatives
- Determine if you're competing on value or price

### Value-Based
- Calculate the economic value your product creates for customers
- Price as a fraction of value delivered
- Use "10x rule": customers should get 10x the value they pay

### Test & Iterate
- A/B test pricing pages
- Offer limited-time pricing experiments
- Track conversion rates at different price points

## Packaging & Tiers

### Good/Better/Best Framework
- **Good (starter)**: Core features, lowest price, entry point
- **Better (growth)**: Most features, designed for the majority
- **Best (enterprise/premium)**: Everything, highest price, for power users

### Feature Gating Decisions
- What's free vs paid? (based on value delivery)
- What separates tiers? (usage limits, features, support level)
- Where's the upgrade trigger? (the moment users naturally want more)

### Anchor Pricing
- Always show the most expensive tier first or alongside others
- Use the top tier to make middle tier feel reasonable
- Highlight the "recommended" or "most popular" tier

## Connection to Growth System

Your pricing directly affects:
- **Target CAC** (from Guardrails): ARPU x Gross Margin x Payback = Target CAC
- **Model-Channel Fit**: Higher ARPU = can afford more expensive channels
- **Monetization Motion**: Product-led needs self-serve pricing; sales-led needs quote-based
- **Retention**: Pricing structure affects churn (annual vs monthly, usage vs flat)

---

## Questions Sequence

1. What value metric best represents customer value? (Feature/Usage/Outcome)
2. What are you specifically charging for?
3. How will you charge? (Flat/Tiered/Per-user/Usage/Hybrid)
4. When will you charge? (Monthly/Annual/Trial/Freemium/Upfront)
5. What do 3-5 customers/prospects say about pricing? (Run WTP questions)
6. What's your initial price band?
7. How many tiers? What separates them?
8. What's free vs paid?
9. What triggers the upgrade?
10. Does your pricing support your Target CAC from Guardrails?
