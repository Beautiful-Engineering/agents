# Story System

The Story System is your messaging foundation — the single narrative that connects your brand to your audience across all channels. It ensures every touchpoint reinforces the same memory, building brand salience (the mental shortcut where your name pops up first when the problem arises).

Messaging is the lowest-cost, highest-leverage growth lever. A copy shift in a headline can increase conversions by 20% in 20 minutes.

---

## Part 1: Core Story

The single narrative thread that explains why you exist, who you help, and how you help them.

### How to Discover Your Core Story

**Step 1: Find the Narrative Kernel**
Ask: "If we disappeared tomorrow, what would people miss most?"
- Not the feature — the pain point customers can't imagine returning to
- Collect 10 real user quotes describing the pain in their own language
- Circle the words that keep showing up

**Step 2: Spot the Enemy**
Every good story has tension. Who/what are you fighting?
- What is the thorn in your customers' sides?
- Framing an enemy clarifies your mission

**Step 3: Elevate from Feature to Outcome**
Customers don't buy features — they buy what features make possible.
- What does life look like before you and after you?
- Early stage: be explicit — anchor on feature AND outcome

**Step 4: Identify Your Differentiation**
- Map competitor claims side by side
- Look for proof only you have: data, integrations, cultural stance, founder POV
- Swap logos test: if your rival could run your story without changes, it's too generic

**Step 5: Validate with the Market**
- Qualitative: Do customers repeat your kernel back in interviews?
- Quantitative: A/B test headlines and ad framings
- Behavioral: Look for organic echo — customers describe you in your words unprompted

### Core Story Formula
"We exist to [solve this problem / defeat this enemy] for [who you help], so they can [achieve this outcome]. Unlike [alternatives], we [unique differentiator]."

### Checklist
- Specific enough to be remembered
- Flexible enough to stretch across products
- True enough that customers nod along
- Differentiated enough that competitors can't claim it
- Validated enough that customers echo it back

## Part 2: Brand Personality

Define the human characteristics your brand embodies. This keeps you sounding like the same brand across all channels.

Questions:
- What 3-5 personality traits describe your brand?
- What tone do you use? (formal vs casual, serious vs playful, etc.)
- What brands do you admire for their voice (even outside your industry)?

---

## Part 3: Personas (JTBD + Pain + Language)

Personas are NOT demographics. They are jobs-to-be-done, pain points, and the exact language people use.

### What to Capture Per Persona
| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Persona Name | Handle that encodes the job (e.g., "Ops Olivia — Unblock approvals") |
| Job to Be Done | What are they "hiring" your product to accomplish? |
| Pain / Frustration | What feels frustrating, costly, or emotionally draining? (specific) |
| Trigger | What moment makes them start looking for a solution? |
| Common Objection | Why would they say no? |
| Exact Words They Use | Verbatim language from interviews/reviews |
| Motivations | What "better" looks like for them |

### How to Choose Personas (Venn Test)
- If jobs, pains, and motivations mostly overlap → share one persona
- If mostly distinct → create separate personas
- If you can't write a single hook for both → they're different personas
- Max 3-4 primary personas. More = diluted story.

### Persona Prioritization Matrix (score 1-5)
- Urgency (pain now)
- Willingness to pay
- TAM/volume
- Reachability
- Strategic fit with core story

## Part 4: Value Propositions

Answer: "Out of all the options available, why should I choose you?"

### 3-Column Value Prop Development
| Bad Alternatives | Value Prop | Top Persona |
|-----------------|-----------|-------------|
| What people resort to without you (and what makes it bad) | How your product offers a better solution | Which persona cares most and why |

### Value Prop Refinement (4-Column)
| Value Prop | Problem (past tense, specific) | Implications ("twist the knife") | Solution + Benefits |
|-----------|-------------------------------|--------------------------------|-------------------|

### Criteria for Good Value Props
- Different from competitors (if they can say it too, it's not yours)
- Relevant to your personas (it has to matter)
- Credible (you can actually deliver)

Target: 3-5 refined value props.

---

## Part 5: Hooks (10 Frameworks)

Hooks package your value props into attention-grabbing bites. Same story, different clothes.

| Framework | Structure |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pain Spotlight | "Tired of [painful status quo]?" |
| Aspirational Outcome | "Imagine if [positive end state]." |
| Persona Call-Out | "[Role], this is for you: [benefit]." |
| Credibility / Proof | "[X people] already [achieve benefit] with us." |
| Contrarian / Rebel | "Forget [industry cliche]. Do [better way]." |
| Speed / Ease | "From [pain] to [result] in [timeframe]." |
| Stakes / Urgency | "Every [timeframe] you waste on [pain], you lose [outcome]." |
| Cultural / Trend | "[Trend] is everywhere. Here's the smarter way." |
| Obstacle Flip | "Hate [annoyance]? That's why we built [product]." |
| Metaphor / Analogy | "[Product] is like [familiar thing], but for [domain]." |

For each value prop: write 3-4 quick drafts per framework, filter to 5-8 evergreen hooks.

## Part 6: Funnel Copy & CTAs

### Funnel Messaging Map
| Stage | Customer Mindset | Messaging Focus |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Top (Awareness) | "I have this pain but don't know the fix" | Hook on pain/problem or tease aspirational outcome |
| Mid (Consideration) | "I'm weighing options" | Show how you solve it, why you're different, address objections |
| Bottom (Decision) | "I'm almost ready, need confidence" | Direct CTA, urgency, reassurance |
| Post (Retention) | "I signed up, was it worth it?" | Reinforce promise through product value + lifecycle messaging |

### CTA Guidelines by Stage
- **Top**: Low-barrier ("See how it works", "Take the quiz", "Get the free guide")
- **Mid**: Experience-based ("Start free trial", "Explore templates", "Compare plans")
- **Bottom**: Action-oriented ("Get started today", "Join 10K+ teams", "Buy now, risk free")
- **Post**: Engagement-driven ("Set up your first project", "Invite your team")

Rule: Never leave it up to the customer. A confused mind always says "no."

## Questions Sequence

1. What is your narrative kernel — if you disappeared, what would people miss most?
2. Who/what is the enemy your customers fight?
3. What does life look like before and after your product?
4. What can you credibly claim that competitors can't?
5. What 3-5 personality traits describe your brand voice?
6. Who are your 2-3 primary personas? (JTBD, pains, triggers, objections, language)
7. What are the bad alternatives your personas resort to?
8. What are your 3-5 value propositions? (bad alternative → value prop → top persona)
9. For each value prop: what's the specific problem, implications, solution, benefits?
10. Generate 5-8 hooks using the 10 frameworks
11. Map your messaging across the funnel (awareness → consideration → decision → retention)
12. What are your CTAs at each funnel stage?
