## The #1 Rule: Targeting Is 90% of Success

Most cold email campaigns fail not because of bad copy but because they're emailing the wrong people. Before writing a single word, spend 80% of your time on targeting.

**Ask yourself:** "If I could only email 10 people, who would they be and why?" Start there and expand.

## Ladder of Product Awareness (Prioritize in This Order)

Target prospects in this order — people higher on the ladder are closer to buying:

| Priority | Awareness Level | Signal | Why They're Valuable |
|----------|----------------|--------|---------------------|
| 1 (highest) | Dissatisfied competitor users | Negative reviews, churned accounts, complaints | Already bought into the solution category; actively looking for alternatives |
| 2 | Active competitor users | Using a competitor today | Understand the problem; may be open to switching if approached well |
| 3 | Evaluating solutions | Searching, comparing, attending demos | In active buying mode; timing is critical |
| 4 | Problem-aware | Know they have the problem but haven't shopped | Need education before selling; longer sales cycle |
| 5 (lowest) | Unaware | Don't know they have the problem | Avoid these for cold email — use content marketing instead |

## Defining Your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Interview Existing Customers First

Before building any list, interview 5-10 of your best customers. Ask:

1. What value props resonate most with you?
2. What's the single biggest improvement you've seen since using us?
3. What finally got you to purchase/sign up?
4. What alternatives did you evaluate and reject? Why?
5. How did you first hear about us?
6. What's your exact job title and who do you report to?
7. What would have made you say no?

**Use their exact language in your emails.** Customer interviews are the single best source of email copy.

### ICP Template

```markdown
## Ideal Customer Profile

### Demographics
- Job titles: [list decision-makers AND champions/influencers]
- Company size: [employee count range]
- Industry: [specific verticals]
- Geography: [if relevant]
- Revenue range: [if B2B]
- Stage: [startup, growth, enterprise]

### Technographics
- Tools they use: [signals that indicate fit]
- Platforms: [where they hang out online]
- Tech stack indicators: [BuiltWith signals]

### Psychographics
- Core pain point: [in their words, from customer interviews]
- Current workaround: [what they do today without your product]
- Trigger events: [what makes them ready to buy NOW]
- Objections: [top 3 reasons they'd say no]

### Disqualifiers (Do NOT email)
- [Company too small/large]
- [Wrong industry]
- [Already a customer]
- [Competitor employee]
```

## Critical Event Triggers

These events signal a prospect may be ready to buy NOW. Prioritize prospects experiencing these:

### High-Urgency Triggers
- **Major crisis**: A fire that needs solving immediately (data breach, system outage, key employee departure)
- **New budget authority**: Just got promoted, new budget cycle, new funding round
- **Team restructuring**: New hire in a relevant role, department expansion
- **Technology migration**: Switching platforms, sunsetting tools
- **Regulatory change**: New compliance requirements in their industry

### Medium-Urgency Triggers
- **Job postings**: Hiring for roles related to your product's function (signal they're investing in the area)
- **Conference attendance**: At an event related to your problem space
- **Content engagement**: Published or shared content about the problem you solve
- **LinkedIn activity**: Profile changes, new certifications, career transitions
- **Company milestones**: Series A/B funding, IPO prep, acquisition, new market entry

### How to Monitor Triggers
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**: Saved searches with job change alerts
- **Google Alerts**: Company names, competitor names, industry keywords
- **Crunchbase / PitchBook**: Funding round alerts
- **Job board monitoring**: Indeed, LinkedIn Jobs for relevant role postings
- **Glassdoor reviews**: Dissatisfied employees (signal internal problems your product might solve)

## Lead Sourcing Strategies

### 1. LinkedIn Sales Navigator (Primary for B2B)
- Boolean search by title + industry + company size + geography
- Save searches for ongoing lead pipeline
- Export to Apollo/Hunter for email enrichment
- Use "Spotlights" filter for job changes (trigger events)

### 2. Email Lookup Services (Scalable)
- **Apollo.io**: Largest B2B database, integrated email finding + sending
- **Hunter.io**: Domain-based email lookup, bulk verification
- **Clearbit**: Enrichment + advertising (pre-targeting)
- **ContactOut**: Chrome extension for LinkedIn email extraction
- **Expect ~10% error rate** — always verify before sending

### 3. Email Guessing (Predictable Formats)
Most companies follow a pattern. Test these:
1. `firstname@company.com`
2. `firstname.lastname@company.com`
3. `first.last@company.com`
4. `flastname@company.com`

**Validation methods:**
- Gmail compose: hover over the email address to see if a Google profile appears
- Google search: `"email" site:company.com "firstname"`
- Test with Mailinator or similar service

### 4. Community Mining
- **Reddit**: Search for problem-related discussions, DM active participants
- **Slack communities**: Join industry Slack groups, note active members
- **Discord servers**: Similar to Slack — observe before outreach
- **Forums / Quora**: Find people asking questions your product answers

### 5. Conference & Event Lists
- Attendee lists (often published or available for purchase)
- Speaker lists from relevant events
- Sponsor company employees
- "I saw you at [event]" is a strong personalization angle

### 6. Competitor Customer Identification
- **G2/Capterra reviews**: Reviewers are confirmed competitor users
- **Case studies**: Companies featured on competitor websites
- **Social mentions**: `@competitor` mentions on Twitter/LinkedIn
- **Job postings**: "Experience with [competitor tool]" in job descriptions
- **Integration pages**: Companies listed as using competitor integrations

### 7. Advanced Hacks
- **Twitter search**: Advanced search with "at" and "dot" keywords to find publicly shared emails
- **Newsletter subscriptions**: Subscribe to target's newsletter, reply to get personal email
- **Facebook group mining**: Cross-reference group members with LinkedIn profiles
- **GitHub scraping**: For developer/designer contacts
- **Wholesale databases**: usbizdata.com for bulk business contact data

## Email Verification Workflow

**ALWAYS verify before sending. Bounces destroy sender reputation.**

### Process
1. Collect emails into spreadsheet
2. Run through verification service:
   - **Bouncer** (recommended): bulk list cleaning
   - **NeverBounce**: real-time verification API
   - **Hunter.io**: verification included with lookup
3. Remove all "invalid" and "risky" results
4. Only send to "valid" / "deliverable" addresses
5. Target: **<3% bounce rate** on every campaign

### Verification Categories
- **Valid**: Safe to send — confirmed deliverable
- **Risky**: May bounce — include only if list is small and well-targeted
- **Invalid**: Will bounce — NEVER send
- **Unknown**: Cannot determine — treat as risky

## Funnel Math: Working Backward from Revenue

Use this to calculate how many leads you need. Adjust rates based on your actual data.

### Starting Assumptions (B2B)
| Step | Conversion Rate | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| Emails sent → Opens | 30-50% | Depends on subject line + deliverability |
| Opens → Replies | 10-20% of sends | Depends on body copy + targeting |
| Replies → Meetings booked | 40-60% of positive replies | Depends on CTA + qualification |
| Meetings → Deals closed | 20-50% | Depends on product-market fit + price |

### Example Calculation
**Goal**: 10 new customers this month, $5K deal value = $50K revenue

Working backward:
- Need 10 closes → at 30% close rate → need ~33 meetings
- Need 33 meetings → at 50% reply-to-meeting rate → need ~66 positive replies
- Need 66 positive replies → at 10% reply rate → need ~660 emails sent
- 660 emails ÷ 22 working days = **30 emails/day**

**This tells you**: You need 1 sending account doing 30/day, or 2 accounts doing 15/day each.

## Pre-Targeting Strategy (Advanced)

**Concept:** Run ads to your lead list for 1-2 weeks BEFORE sending the first email.

### Why It Works
- Increases legitimacy (companies big enough to advertise seem more established)
- Boosts open rates 20-50% (name recognition)
- Best for conservative industries: Government, Construction, Healthcare, Manufacturing, Real Estate

### How to Test
1. Split lead list into Group A and Group B (500-1,000 each)
2. Upload Group A to LinkedIn/Facebook as a custom audience
3. Run targeted ads to Group A for 7-10 days (aim for 7+ frequency)
4. Email both Group A and Group B with identical copy
5. Compare open and reply rates

### Tools for Pre-Targeting
- **Clearbit Advertising**: Match work emails → social profiles for ad targeting
- **LinkedIn Matched Audiences**: Upload company/contact list directly
- **Facebook Custom Audiences**: Upload email list for Meta ad targeting
- **Primer**: Budget-friendly alternative to Clearbit
