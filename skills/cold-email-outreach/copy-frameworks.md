## The Copy Rule

A cold email has 3 seconds to earn a reply. Most get skimmed — not read. Write for the skim: short, specific, and impossible to ignore.

**Under 5 sentences performs best.** If your email needs scrolling on mobile, it's too long.

## Email Archetypes (A/B Test 2-3)

Create 2-3 distinctly different email approaches. Not minor variations — completely different angles and tones. Test each on ~100 prospects before scaling the winner.

### Archetype 1: Humble & Inquisitive
**Thesis**: Position yourself as a curious peer seeking to understand, not sell.

**Tone**: Respectful, genuinely interested, low-pressure.

**Template**:
```
Subject: Quick question about {{company}}'s approach to [topic]

Hi {{firstName}},

I noticed {{personalization — something specific about their work}}.

Curious — how are you currently handling [specific problem your product solves]?

We've been helping [similar companies] with [brief value prop], and I'd love to hear how {{company}} thinks about it.

Worth a 10-minute chat?

{{yourName}}
```

### Archetype 2: Value-First
**Thesis**: Lead with a specific insight, resource, or data point. Give before you ask.

**Tone**: Educational, generous, authoritative.

**Template**:
```
Subject: [Specific insight] for {{company}}

Hi {{firstName}},

I was looking at {{personalization — their website, product, content}} and noticed [specific observation].

We found that [relevant insight/stat] — [1-sentence explanation of why it matters to them].

I put together [resource/analysis/benchmark] that might be useful for {{company}}. Want me to send it over?

{{yourName}}
```

### Archetype 3: Direct & Honest
**Thesis**: Cut the pleasantries. State why you're reaching out plainly and make a direct ask.

**Tone**: Straightforward, confident, no-BS.

**Template**:
```
Subject: [Problem] at {{company}}

Hi {{firstName}},

I help [role/industry] companies [achieve specific outcome].

[One sentence of social proof: "We helped X do Y in Z time."]

If [specific problem] is on your radar, I'd love to show you how we approach it. 15 minutes?

{{yourName}}
```

### Archetype 4: SPARC Method (For Partnerships & Strategic Outreach)
**Thesis**: Position as a potential collaborator, not a seller. Lead with mutual value.

**S — Signal Recognition**: Email when targets show openness (new launch, partnership announcement, funding round, speaking engagement).

**P — Partnership Positioning**: Reframe the ask as mutual exploration, not extraction.

**A — Authentic Connection**: Demonstrate thorough research (reference specific content, achievements, or initiatives).

**R — Reciprocal Value**: Lead with what YOU offer them (content, data, access, introductions, cross-promotion).

**C — Clear Next Step**: Low-friction ask ("Worth exploring?" not "Let me schedule a 45-minute demo").

**Template**:
```
Subject: Partnership idea: [specific benefit for them]

Hi {{firstName}},

{{Signal: Reference their recent activity/content/achievement.}}

We're working on [brief description] that could align with [their recent initiative].

Specifically, we could [concrete value proposition for them — what they get].

We've already [relevant proof: traction, existing partnerships, results].

Worth exploring? Happy to share more details.

{{yourName}}
```

## Subject Line Formulas

### Rules
- **Under 50 characters** (mobile optimization — subject gets truncated after 50)
- **Informal tone** (distinguish from spam/marketing emails)
- **Self-evident** (reader knows what the email is about without opening — unless deliberately testing opaque/curiosity-driven lines)
- **No spam trigger words**: URGENT, FREE, Act Now, Limited Time, Congratulations, Winner

### Good Subject Lines
| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Specific question | `Quick question about {{company}}'s [topic]` |
| Mutual connection | `{{mutualContact}} suggested I reach out` |
| Observation | `Noticed [specific thing] about {{company}}` |
| Value tease | `Idea for {{company}}'s [initiative]` |
| Social proof | `How [similar company] solved [problem]` |
| Curiosity (opaque) | `[Topic]` (single word — high open rate but lower reply rate) |
| Direct | `[Problem] at {{company}}` |
| Partnership | `Collaboration idea: [specific benefit]` |

### Bad Subject Lines (Never Use)
- "Request to connect" — vague, sounds like LinkedIn spam
- "Let's hop on the phone" — presumptuous, high commitment
- "Check this out" — clickbait, no context
- "URGENT" — spam trigger
- "Following up" — as a first email subject line
- "Are you the right person?" — signals you didn't research
- "I'd love to pick your brain" — asks without offering

### A/B Testing Subjects
Test 2-3 subject lines per archetype across 50-100 emails each:
- **Transparent vs. opaque**: "How X solved [problem]" vs. "[Topic]"
- **Question vs. statement**: "How do you handle [X]?" vs. "[X] for {{company}}"
- **Personal vs. business**: "{{firstName}}, quick question" vs. "[Problem] at {{company}}"

## Body Copy Structure (Max 5 Sentences)

### The 5-Part Formula

**1. Hook the reader (Line 1 — most critical)**

Three approaches:
- **Ask a question**: "Curious if you're still managing {{process}} with {{current tool}}?"
- **Address a pain point**: "I noticed {{company}} is using 6 different vendors for {{function}}."
- **Demonstrate value**: "Want to complete {{task}} in 2-3 days instead of 2-3 weeks?"

**Rules for Line 1:**
- Must be relevant to THEM (not about you)
- Must signal you did research (not a mass email)
- Must create an information gap or address a real concern

**2. Why you're reaching out (Line 2-3)**

- Show you value what they value (reference their content, company mission, recent work)
- Connect your outreach to something specific about them
- Avoid: "I'm reaching out because I'm a [title] at [company]..." — nobody cares about your title

**3. Handle the #1 objection (Line 3-4)**

- From customer interviews, identify the top objection prospects have
- Address it subtly without raising it as a question
- Use social proof for trust objections ("We're already working with [similar company]")
- Use specificity for value objections ("Reduced [metric] by 40% in 3 weeks")

**Important**: Only handle 1-2 objections. Addressing too many objections can RAISE objections that the reader hadn't thought of.

**4. Personal touch (Line 4)**

Two approaches:
- **Work-relevant**: "I saw your talk at [event] about [topic] — [genuine reaction]"
- **Personal**: A non-work connection that builds rapport (same university, shared interest, mutual connection)

Pick ONE approach. Don't force both.

**5. One clear CTA (Line 5)**

- Low-investment next step
- Action-oriented language
- Specific and easy to say yes to

| Good CTAs | Bad CTAs |
|-----------|----------|
| "Worth a 10-minute chat?" | "Let me know if you want to set up a 45-minute demo" |
| "Should I send over details?" | "Would love to tell you all about our platform" |
| "Open to seeing how X works?" | "When are you free for a call this week?" |
| "Interested in seeing [resource]?" | "Let me know your thoughts" (vague, no clear action) |

## Follow-Up Sequence (Max 3 Total Touches)

Most replies come from follow-ups, NOT the initial email. But there's a limit — after 3 touches, you become a pest.

### Follow-Up 1 (3 days after initial)
**Goal**: Add new value, not just "checking in"

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi {{firstName}},

Following up with something that might be relevant —
[New piece of value: case study, data point, article, benchmark, or insight].

[One sentence connecting it to their situation].

Thoughts?

{{yourName}}
```

### Follow-Up 2 (7 days after Follow-Up 1)
**Goal**: Different angle, final attempt, easy to respond to

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi {{firstName}},

Last note from me — I know you're busy.

[Reframe the value proposition from a different angle than the initial email].

If [problem] isn't a priority right now, no worries at all. But if it is, I'd love to help.

Either way, appreciate your time.

{{yourName}}
```

### Follow-Up Rules
- Always reply to the original thread (same subject line with "Re:")
- Each follow-up MUST add new value (don't just say "bumping this" or "checking in")
- End each follow-up with a question (questions get more replies than statements)
- After 3 touches with no response, stop. You can recycle the lead after 5-6 months.
- If they respond negatively, stop immediately and respect the opt-out

## Personalization Framework

### The Personalization Spectrum

| Level | Effort | Example | When to Use |
|-------|--------|---------|------------|
| Zero (mail merge) | None | "Hi {{firstName}}" only | Never — this is spam |
| Low | 30 seconds | Company name + industry reference | High-volume, lower-value targets |
| Medium | 2-3 minutes | Specific observation about their company/role | Standard for most outreach |
| High | 5-10 minutes | Reference specific content, achievements, mutual connections | High-value targets, C-suite, partnerships |

### What to Personalize (and What Not To)

**Always personalize:**
- First line (specific observation about them/their company)
- The problem you're referencing (make it relevant to their situation)
- Social proof (mention companies in their industry or size range)

**Don't waste time personalizing:**
- Compliments about their website design (everyone says this)
- Generic LinkedIn profile observations ("I see you're a marketing professional")
- Anything that sounds like it came from a template with variables

### Personalization Research Sources
1. **Their LinkedIn** — Recent posts, profile changes, career transitions
2. **Their company blog/news** — Recent launches, hires, announcements
3. **Podcast appearances** — What they said, what they care about
4. **Twitter/X** — What they share, what they complain about
5. **Crunchbase** — Funding, team size, growth signals
6. **Their product** — Use it, form genuine opinions
7. **Mutual connections** — Check LinkedIn for shared contacts

### Personalization Sentence Templates

```
"I saw your [post/talk/article] about [topic] — [specific, genuine reaction]."

"Noticed {{company}} just [trigger event]. Curious how that's affecting [relevant area]."

"I've been using {{product}} for [task] and noticed [specific observation]."

"{{mutualContact}} mentioned you're thinking about [relevant topic]."

"Your [recent hire for X role / job posting for Y] caught my eye — it seems like [inference about their priorities]."
```

## Copy Quality Checklist

Before sending any email, run through this checklist:

### Harry Dry's 3 Rules
- [ ] **Can you visualize it?** Concrete and specific beats abstract. "Save 4 hours/week on reporting" not "improve your workflow efficiency."
- [ ] **Can you falsify it?** Can the claim be proven true or false? "We helped Company X grow revenue 40%" not "We deliver results."
- [ ] **Can nobody else say this?** Is this unique to you? "Only tool that integrates with [specific system]" not "Best solution on the market."

### Demand Curve Cold Email Checklist
- [ ] Under 5 sentences total
- [ ] First line is about THEM, not you
- [ ] No spam trigger words (free, urgent, act now, limited)
- [ ] One clear, low-friction CTA
- [ ] Social proof included (subtly)
- [ ] Top objection handled
- [ ] Plain text (no HTML, no images, minimal links)
- [ ] Would YOU reply to this email?
- [ ] Sounds like a human wrote it to one person (not a mass email)
- [ ] Subject line under 50 characters
- [ ] No more than 1 link in the body
- [ ] Unsubscribe/opt-out mechanism available
