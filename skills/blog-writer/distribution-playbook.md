# Distribution Playbook

Repurposing frameworks, social media snippets, email excerpts, community posting rules, and content distribution channels.

Source: `content-marketing-fundamentals/07-content-distribution.md`, `content-marketing-fundamentals/05-editorial-virality.md`

---

## Core Principle

There are two parts to successful content marketing: **creation and distribution**. Without distribution, even the best content won't reach its audience.

---

## Distribution Plan Template

Generate this after the blog post is finalized:

```markdown
# Distribution Plan — [Post Title]

## Social Media Snippets

### LinkedIn
[Hook adapted for LinkedIn — professional angle, can be longer. Use Identity or Credibility hook. Include a question to drive comments.]

### Twitter/X
[Hook adapted for Twitter — concise, punchy. Use Surprise or Counter-Narrative hook. Thread-friendly format — first tweet hooks, subsequent tweets deliver value.]

### Reddit
[Identify 2-3 relevant subreddits. Frame as genuine contribution, NOT promotion. Follow the 9:1 rule — 9 community contributions for every 1 self-promotion. Lead with the insight, not the link.]

## Email Newsletter Excerpt
[2-3 sentence teaser that hooks subscribers into clicking through. Reference the most surprising or valuable insight from the post.]

## Repurposing Ideas
- [ ] Twitter/X thread (break post into 8-12 tweet thread)
- [ ] LinkedIn carousel (extract key frameworks/lists into visual slides)
- [ ] Short-form video script (60-90 seconds summarizing the key insight)
- [ ] Infographic (visual summary of data/frameworks from the post)
- [ ] Podcast talking points (if applicable)

## Community Distribution
- [ ] [Subreddit 1] — angle: [how to frame it]
- [ ] [Subreddit 2] — angle: [how to frame it]
- [ ] [Relevant Slack/Discord group] — angle: [how to frame it]
- [ ] Hacker News (if technical content)
- [ ] Product Hunt (if product-related)
- [ ] Quora (answer a related question, reference the post)

## Outreach
- [ ] Relevant newsletters that curate content: [list]
- [ ] Syndicate on Medium (use import feature to preserve canonical URL)

## Paid Amplification (Only if applicable)
- Consider only for high-LTV, complex products where education drives conversion
- Point ads at content (not landing page) only when customers need to read a lot to buy
- Retarget content readers with landing page ads later
```

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Social Media
- **Don't just post a link** — create unique copy for each platform explaining why the content is worth checking out
- **Adapt the hook** to each platform's style and audience
- **Use your personal accounts** if the brand account is small — personal networks have higher engagement
- **Tag relevant topics/hashtags** — make content discoverable

### Email Newsletter
- **Tease, don't summarize** — the goal is a click-through, not a complete recap
- **Use the most compelling insight** from the post as the hook
- **Keep the excerpt short** — 2-3 sentences max before the CTA link
- **Encourage sharing** — ask subscribers to forward to someone who'd benefit

### Online Communities (Reddit, Quora, HN, Slack)
- **Self-promotion is frowned upon** — focus on genuine contribution
- **Reddit 9:1 rule**: Only 1 out of every 10 submissions should be your own content
- **Lead with value** — share the insight, not the link
- **Be upfront about affiliation** — if asked, don't hide that it's your content
- **Match the community's tone** — Reddit is casual, HN is technical, Quora is educational

### Syndication
- **Medium**: Use the import feature (preserves canonical URL so you don't compete with yourself in search). Submit to relevant Medium publications to amplify reach.
- **Other platforms**: Only syndicate where canonical URLs are respected

## Virality Optimization (For High-Impact Posts)

When the post has viral potential, optimize for sharing:

### 7 Virality Tactics (from Demand Curve)

1. **Focus on one channel** — create targeted content for one platform first, repurpose later
2. **Reverse engineer popular content** — analyze what's shared most in your industry. What format? What emotion? What timing?
3. **Leverage company data** — exclusive data that no one else has is inherently shareable
4. **Pinpoint who shares and why** — Status? Identity? Being helpful? Novelty?
5. **Compelling headline** — Stir emotion, create urgency, offer solution, use numbers
6. **Optimize for shareability**:
   - Focus on a single message (don't tackle too many topics)
   - Social share buttons prominent and accessible
   - Easy to consume (headings, lists, line breaks)
   - Descriptive featured image
7. **Authentic to brand** — Don't copy viral styles that don't fit your voice

### Shareability Motivations
People share content for these reasons:
- **Status**: Sharing this makes me look smart/informed
- **Identity**: This reflects who I am
- **Being helpful**: My network would benefit from this
- **Novelty**: This is new and interesting

**Test**: For each post, ask "Who would share this and why?" If you can't answer specifically, the post needs more shareability.

## Leverage Existing Relationships First

The best distribution comes from existing relationships, not cold outreach.

**Prioritize**:
1. Your email subscriber list
2. Your personal social media accounts
3. Brand social media accounts
4. Relationships with other brands/companies
5. Relationships with journalists, influencers, thought leaders
6. Online communities you're already active in

**Cold outreach is last resort** — average response rate is 1-3%. Only use when you've exhausted warmer channels.
