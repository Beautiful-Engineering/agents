# Value Metric Selection

How to identify what customers are paying for — the foundation of every pricing decision.

Source: `monetization-pricing-strategy/02-how-to-design-a-monetization-strategy.md`, `monetization-pricing-strategy/03-what-to-charge-for.md`

---

## Four Common Monetization Mistakes

1. **Pricing too low** — Leaving money on the table and capping growth potential
2. **Charging the wrong way** — E.g. charging per seat when customers would rather pay per usage
3. **Guessing on monetization** — Picking numbers or structures without customer input or testing
4. **Leaving monetization until the last minute** — Rushing to pick a price right before launch

## Four Principles

1. **Monetization isn't set in stone** — You'll refine as you grow and collect more data
2. **Your customers influence pricing** — Their expectations, preferences, and feedback should shape how you design and iterate (model-market fit in action)
3. **Precision isn't the goal** — Early on, land in the right ballpark. What matters is alignment with the rest of the Foundational Five
4. **Action beats theorizing** — Testing and talking to your market beats over-engineering from behind a desk

## Three Value Metric Categories

Your customers pay for one of three things. Revenue should rise in step with the value customers receive.

| Category | What It Means | Examples |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Features unlocked** | Customers pay more as they unlock additional features or higher-tier access | Canva (brand kits, premium templates), Peloton (live classes, performance tracking) |
| **Usage consumed** | Customers pay more the more they use the product | AWS (compute hours, storage), Twilio (messages, API calls) |
| **Outcomes achieved** | Customers pay when the product helps them reach a result | Airbnb (per booking), Ticketmaster (per ticket sold) |

**The rule**: The more of that thing they get, the more they pay.

## How to Identify Your Value Metric

### Step 1: JTBD Translation

Revisit your Job-to-be-Done. Why do customers "hire" your product?

| Company | JTBD | Value Metric |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Airbnb | Connect travelers with places to stay | Nights booked |
| SendGrid | Help companies send email at scale | Emails/month |
| HubSpot | Manage customer relationships | Marketing contacts |
| Wistia | Host and track video content | Videos/podcast episodes |
| Grubhub | Connect diners with restaurants | Orders placed |
| Zendesk | Automate customer support | Automated answers |

### Step 2: Translate JTBD into Something Measurable

The ultimate JTBD may be too abstract to monetize directly. Use **proxies** — things that are measurable, even if they aren't the ultimate outcome.

Three proxy types:
- **Usage proxy** — Number of units consumed (episodes edited, hours processed)
- **Feature proxy** — Access to advanced capabilities (multi-track editing, AI transcription)
- **Outcome proxy** — Measurable results delivered (bookings secured, leads generated)

### Step 3: Pressure-Test Your Options (5-Point Test)

Ask of each possible proxy:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| **Does it align with customers' needs?** | Customers should be paying for the value they experience. Usage should reflect success relative to the JTBD. |
| **Is it scalable?** | Larger customers should get more out of your metric than smaller customers. |
| **Is it clear?** | No uncertainty about what the metric is (customers understand it) and how it's performing (team can track it). Transparent, predictable, easy to measure. |
| **Do price and value scale proportionately?** | If not, susceptible to the "taxi-meter effect" — customers become hyper-aware that using more means spending more without proportional value. |
| **Does it make sense for acquisition?** | The metric shouldn't add friction to the purchase decision. It should help with customer acquisition, not hinder it. |

**Cut any metric that doesn't check all boxes.**

## Deliverable: Value Metric Statement

By the end of this exercise, you should be able to answer in plain English:

```
- "Our customers pay for ____."
- "When they use/get/unlock more of ____, they pay us more."
- Category: [Features / Usage / Outcomes]
- JTBD: [one sentence]
- Proxy: [the measurable thing]
- Pressure test: [pass/fail on all 5 questions]
```
