# Tier Design & Packaging

Good/Better/Best tier design, feature gating, seven SaaS pricing models, paths to live, and offer packaging.

## Seven Common SaaS Pricing Models

| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Per-user / per-seat** | Charge per user or seat | Team-based products with unique per-seat value |
| **Usage-based** | Charge per unit of consumption | Products with clear, measurable usage metrics |
| **Tiered feature** | Good/Better/Best with feature gates | Products with a rich feature set spanning personas |
| **Flat-rate** | One price for everyone | Simple products, early-stage, single persona |
| **Freemium + premium** | Free tier with paid upgrades | Products with clear upgrade triggers |
| **Outcome-based** | Charge per result delivered | Products with measurable customer outcomes |
| **Hybrid** | Combination of above models | Complex products serving multiple segments |

### Starting Point Decision Framework

- **What's the clearest value metric?** (users, usage, outcomes, time saved)
- **How do competitors price?** (opportunity to differentiate or follow proven models)
- **What's familiar to your buyer?** (reduces friction in sales conversations)
- **Can you anchor against something expensive?** (human labor, current solutions, cost of the problem)

## Good/Better/Best Tier Design

### Tier Structure Principles

1. **Max four tiers** — More than that overwhelms buyers
2. **One tier should be "recommended"** — Reduces decision friction
3. **Each tier should have a clear persona** — Who is this tier for?
4. **Upgrade triggers should be obvious** — Users hit a natural limit and want more

### Feature Gating Framework

| Tier | Features | Target | Pricing Signal |
|------|----------|--------|---------------|
| **Free / Starter** | Core value prop only. Enough to experience the JTBD. | Individuals, experimenters | Low friction entry |
| **Pro / Growth** | Power features + higher limits. The "recommended" tier. | SMBs, power users | Sweet spot pricing |
| **Business / Team** | Collaboration, admin controls, integrations | Teams, departments | Per-user or per-seat premium |
| **Enterprise** | Custom SLAs, SSO, dedicated support, variable pricing | Large organizations | Contact sales |

### What Belongs in Each Tier

- **Free tier**: Core functionality that demonstrates value. Don't gate the JTBD — gate the scale.
- **Paid tiers**: Features that correlate with higher value realization (more usage, more users, more advanced workflows)
- **Enterprise**: Security, compliance, custom integrations, SLAs — things that matter to procurement

## Six Paths to Live

Choose based on buyer risk, your proof, and time to first result.

### A) Free Trial
- **Use when**: Time to value is minutes and self-evaluation is realistic
- **Avoid when**: Buyers won't push alone, or value shows only after real setup
- **Set**: 7-14 days, one key action they must take, a decision date

### B) Discounted Trial
- **Use when**: You want commitment while reducing risk
- **Avoid when**: Discounts will anchor expectations low long-term
- **Set**: Short length, clear success check, auto roll-forward terms

### C) Straight Subscription
- **Use when**: Setup is light, proof is strong, the buyer already believes
- **Avoid when**: Value appears only after real setup or change management
- **Set**: Plan, start date, quick checkpoint instead of discounting

### D) Proof of Value (Micro Pilot)
- **Use when**: Buyer wants a tiny, guided test before paid commitment
- **Avoid when**: You need services or integrations to show value
- **Set**: One workflow, one metric, 10-14 days, scheduled readout

### E) Paid Pilot
- **Use when**: There's setup, the buyer has risk, results in 30-45 days
- **Avoid when**: Setup is trivial and belief is high, or pilot fee triggers procurement
- **Set**: Length, 1-2 metrics, what's included, conversion terms, decision meeting on calendar

### F) Design Partnership
- **Use when**: Product is early and you need depth with 1-2 real users
- **Avoid when**: Buyer pushes bespoke features that won't generalize
- **Set**: Start/end date, weekly calls, specific feedback, case study rights, pre-agreed path to paid

## Offer Packaging

### What Customers Get for the Price

Make this a short block the buyer can repeat:

1. **Usage limits** — Credits, messages, contacts. Say the meter plainly.
2. **Seats, if relevant** — Only include if buyer thinks in seats.
3. **Included touches** — Setup call, review, tune-up. Keep small.
4. **What's NOT included** — Call out surprises (custom integrations, managed services).
5. **Success metric** — One line on what success looks like.

### Public Pricing or Not

- **Publish pricing**: SMB or mid-market motions where speed matters
- **"Starts at" pricing**: When scoping varies, keeps discovery honest
- **Keep pricing private**: Custom deals or security-heavy, but give a firm "from" anchor on calls

## Objection Handling at the Pricing Conversation

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Price pushback** | Rerun value math with their numbers. Reduce scope before price. Offer lower volume band rather than discount. |
| **"Can we try it free?"** | 7-14 day proof of value with one action and one metric. Ask for commitment in return (midpoint + wrap-up calls). |
| **"I need to loop in my boss"** | Confirm who signs. "If we get them on board, any other barriers?" Surface every blocker now. |
| **"We would use it if it had X"** | Treat as product signal. If multiple buyers ask, useful. If one buyer, be cautious. Park custom requests unless they generalize. |
| **Wrong persona** | Ask who owns the problem, ask for intro, pivot to discovery with their view of impact. |
