# Pricing Page Copy

Structure, tier comparison template, FAQ template, and common mistakes for pricing pages.

Source: Cross-referenced from landing page curriculum (`make-high-converting-landing-pages/`), copywriting fundamentals (`copywriting-fundamentals/`), and story system (`create-your-story-system/`)

## Pricing Page Structure

A pricing page is a specialized landing page. It follows the same conversion formula:

**Purchase Rate = (Desire + Confidence) / (Effort + Confusion)**

### Standard Pricing Page Sections

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Header** | State who the page is for + what they get. Not a slogan. |
| 2 | **Tier Comparison Table** | Side-by-side plan comparison. One tier visually recommended. |
| 3 | **Feature Breakdown** | Detailed feature-by-feature comparison across tiers |
| 4 | **Social Proof** | Logos, testimonials, or customer count. Immediately after tiers. |
| 5 | **FAQ** | Handle pricing objections, billing questions, comparison questions |
| 6 | **CTA Section** | Final push — reiterate strongest value prop + clear next step |

---

## Pricing Page Header

The header sets context before the visitor sees numbers.

**Do**:
- State who the pricing is for: "Pricing for teams that ship fast"
- Include a value-oriented subheader: "Plans that grow with your usage"
- Be specific about what's included at every level

**Don't**:
- Use generic headers: "Our Pricing" / "Choose Your Plan"
- Lead with the cheapest price as a hook (sets wrong anchor)

## Tier Comparison Template

### Layout Principles

1. **3-4 tiers maximum** — More overwhelms. Less feels limiting.
2. **One tier must be visually recommended** — Badge, color highlight, or "Most Popular" label
3. **Recommended tier should be the middle or second-highest** — Not the cheapest
4. **Annual pricing shown by default** — With monthly toggle available
5. **Enterprise tier uses "Contact Sales"** — Not a listed price

### Tier Card Elements

Each tier card should include:
- **Tier name** — Descriptive, not clever (Starter, Pro, Business, Enterprise)
- **Price** — Large, prominent. Monthly price with annual discount shown.
- **Who it's for** — One line: "For individuals getting started" / "For growing teams"
- **CTA button** — Specific to each tier ("Start Free Trial" / "Upgrade to Pro" / "Contact Sales")
- **Key features** — 5-8 bullet points. Lead with the most differentiating feature.
- **Limits** — Be transparent about caps (users, storage, API calls)

### Feature List Best Practices

- **Lead with benefits, not feature names**: "Unlimited team members" not "Team Management"
- **Use checkmarks for included, dashes for excluded** — Visual scanning
- **Group features by category** — Core, Advanced, Admin/Security, Support
- **Bold the differentiating features** — What makes this tier worth upgrading to
- **Include tooltips for complex features** — Don't make them leave the page

## Pricing FAQ Template

### 7 Questions Every Pricing Page FAQ Should Answer

| # | Question | Purpose |
|---|----------|---------|
| 1 | **What's included in each plan?** | Clarity — restate key differences |
| 2 | **Can I switch plans later?** | Reduce commitment anxiety |
| 3 | **Is there a free trial?** | Lower friction for hesitant buyers |
| 4 | **What happens if I exceed my limits?** | Address taxi-meter anxiety |
| 5 | **Do you offer discounts?** (annual, startup, nonprofit) | Capture price-sensitive segments |
| 6 | **How does billing work?** (when charged, refunds, cancellation) | Reduce effort/confusion |
| 7 | **How is [your product] different from [competitor]?** | Handle the comparison objection directly |

### FAQ Copy Rules

- **Answer the actual question in the first sentence** — Don't hedge
- **Include a specific detail** — Numbers, timelines, concrete policies
- **End with a next step when possible** — "Start a 14-day trial to see for yourself"
- **Don't use FAQ to hide information** — If it's important, it should be on the main page too

## Trust Elements for Pricing Pages

Place these near the tier table to increase confidence:

1. **Money-back guarantee** — "30-day full refund, no questions asked"
2. **No credit card required** (for free trials) — Reduces sign-up friction dramatically
3. **Security badges** — SOC 2, GDPR, encryption. Especially for B2B.
4. **Customer count or logo bar** — "Trusted by 5,000+ companies"
5. **Testimonial quote** — One quote from a customer about value/ROI near the CTA

## Common Pricing Page Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Too many tiers** | Decision paralysis | Max 4 tiers, recommend one |
| **No recommended tier** | Buyer doesn't know where to start | Visually highlight the best-fit option |
| **Feature overload** | Can't tell tiers apart | Lead with 5-8 key features, link to full comparison |
| **Vague tier names** | "Silver/Gold/Platinum" tells nothing | Use descriptive names: Starter, Pro, Business |
| **Hidden pricing** | Frustrates buyers; wastes sales time on unqualified leads | Publish at least "starts at" pricing |
| **No FAQ** | Leaves pricing objections unaddressed | Cover the 7 essential questions |
| **Annual-only pricing** | Creates friction for budget-conscious buyers | Show monthly with annual savings highlighted |
| **Missing trust signals** | Low confidence near the conversion point | Add guarantee, security badges, social proof |
