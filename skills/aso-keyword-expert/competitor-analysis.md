# Competitor Analysis for ASO & Keywords

Frameworks for analyzing competitor apps to find keyword gaps, positioning opportunities, and listing optimization insights. Source: Demand Curve ASA curriculum + growth system competitive research principles.

---

## Competitor Identification

### Step 1: Direct Competitors (Same JTBD)
Apps that solve the same job-to-be-done. These are your Competitor campaign keyword targets.

**How to find them**:
- Search App Store for your core value prop
- Note the top 10 results
- Check "You Might Also Like" on your own listing
- Ask users: "What did you use before our app?"

### Step 2: Indirect Competitors (Adjacent Solutions)
Apps that solve a related problem or serve overlapping audiences. These inform Generic keyword strategy.

**How to find them**:
- Search broader category terms
- Check App Store category rankings
- Look at "Related Searches" in App Store

### Step 3: ASA Competitors (Bidding on Your Terms)
Apps actively running Apple Search Ads on your keywords.

**How to check**:
- Search your core keywords in the App Store
- Note any apps with the "Ad" tag
- Search your own brand name — are competitors bidding on it?

## Competitor Listing Analysis

For each top 3-5 competitors, document:

### Listing Elements
| Element | What to Extract |
|---------|----------------|
| Title | Format, keywords included, character count |
| Subtitle | Value prop framing, keywords included |
| Screenshots | Headline style, messaging angles, visual quality |
| App Preview | Has video? Length, production quality |
| Rating | Star rating, total review count |
| Description | First 3 lines (hook), feature positioning |
| Category | Primary and secondary categories |
| Last Updated | How actively maintained |

### Keyword Intelligence
| Method | What It Reveals |
|--------|----------------|
| Title keywords | Their highest-priority keywords (strongest ranking signal) |
| Subtitle keywords | Their secondary keyword targets |
| Screenshot headlines | The value props they think convert best |
| Description text | Additional keyword targets and feature positioning |

## Keyword Gap Analysis

Compare your keyword list against competitor coverage:

### Step 1: Build Competitor Keyword Map
For each competitor, extract keywords from:
- Title (highest signal)
- Subtitle (high signal)
- Screenshot headlines (conversion-focused terms)
- Description (lower signal but informative)

### Step 2: Identify Gaps
| Gap Type | What It Means | Action |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| Keywords they target that you don't | Potential missed opportunities | Add to Generic campaign and test |
| Keywords you target that they don't | Potential low-competition wins | Prioritize — lower bids may work |
| Keywords both target | Competitive battleground | Need strong listing to convert |
| Keywords neither targets | Untapped opportunity or low volume | Test via Discovery campaign |

### Step 3: Competitive Positioning
Based on the gap analysis, determine your positioning:

| Position | When | Strategy |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Differentiated** | You solve a different pain point | Focus on unique keywords competitors ignore |
| **Direct challenger** | Same solution, better execution | Bid on competitor brand terms + emphasize advantages |
| **Niche specialist** | Same category, specific audience | Focus on long-tail keywords with audience qualifiers |
| **Premium alternative** | Higher quality, higher price | Emphasize quality signals in screenshots and reviews |

## Creative Benchmarking

Compare your App Store listing quality against competitors:

### Screenshot Comparison
For each competitor, rate:
- [ ] Headline clarity (do headlines communicate benefits?)
- [ ] Visual quality (professional or amateur?)
- [ ] Messaging differentiation (unique angles or generic?)
- [ ] Social proof inclusion (ratings, user counts?)
- [ ] CTA presence (final screenshot drives action?)

### What to Steal (Ethically)
- Messaging angles that work across multiple competitors (validated by the market)
- Screenshot layout patterns that look polished
- Value prop framing that you haven't tried
- Feature emphasis patterns (what do top apps highlight first?)

### What to Differentiate
- If all competitors use the same screenshot style, try something different
- If all competitors lead with the same feature, lead with your unique angle
- If competitors have weak reviews, emphasize your higher rating
- If competitors lack video, create one for competitive advantage

## Competitive Intelligence Tools

### Free Options
- **App Store search** — Manual but always current
- **AppAnnie/data.ai (free tier)** — Basic ranking and category data
- **SensorTower (free trial)** — Keyword estimates and competitor keywords

### Paid Options (for scale)
- **MobileAction** — Competitor keyword spying, ASA intelligence
- **AppFollow** — Review monitoring, keyword tracking
- **SearchAds.com** — ASA-specific competitive intelligence

### DIY Approach (Always Works)
1. Search 20-30 relevant keywords in the App Store
2. Note which competitors appear consistently
3. Note which competitors run ads
4. Screenshot their listings for reference
5. Track weekly: do rankings change? Do new competitors appear?

## Competitor Monitoring Cadence

| Frequency | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| Weekly | Are competitors bidding on your brand terms? Any new ASA competitors? |
| Monthly | Competitor listing changes (new screenshots, title updates) |
| Quarterly | Full competitive landscape review — new entrants, exits, positioning shifts |
