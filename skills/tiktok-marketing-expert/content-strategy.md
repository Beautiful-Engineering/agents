# Content Strategy

Tactical content creation frameworks for TikTok — hook formulas, CTA types, proven formats, image generation tips, and performance tracking. Based on data from 500M+ views across multiple accounts.

## Hook Formulas

### The Winning Formula
```
[Another person] + [conflict or doubt] → showed them [app/result] → they changed their mind
```

This works because it creates a **story in the viewer's head** — they picture the other person's reaction. It's about the HUMAN MOMENT, not the app features.

### Proven Examples (with results)
- "My landlord said I can't change anything so I showed her what AI thinks it could look like" → **234,000 views**
- "I showed my mum what AI thinks our living room could be" → **167,000 views**
- "My landlord wouldn't let me decorate until I showed her these" → **147,000 views**

Every post following this formula clears 50K minimum. Most clear 100K.

### What Failed (and why)
- "Why does my flat look like a student loan" → **905 views** (self-focused, no story)
- "See your room in 12+ styles before you commit" → **879 views** (feature-focused, no human)
- "The difference between $500 and $5000 taste" → **2,671 views** (no conflict, no other person)

**Why failures failed:** Self-focused. Talking about the app's features. Nobody cares about YOUR problems.

**Why winners won:** Creates a tiny story. You picture the other person's reaction. The human moment, not the app.

### Hook Brainstorm Rule
Always ask: **"Who's the other person, and what's the conflict?"** If there isn't one, the hook probably won't work.

### Other Proven Hook Patterns
- `"POV: [relatable scenario]"` — drives curiosity and shares
- `"Found the cutest app for [audience]"` — UGC discovery feel
- `"May this type of [emotion] find you"` — aspirational, drives saves
- `"[Person] didn't believe me until I showed them this"` — conflict + reveal

## CTA Strategy

Every video needs at least ONE way for viewers to find your app. Four types, use 1-2 per video max.

### The 4 CTA Types

| Type | How It Works | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Caption CTA** | Mention the business naturally in the caption | "Do these hobbies and 1000+ more on our app 'Curiosity Quench'" |
| **Pinned comment** | Pin the first comment with the CTA. When a video blows up, people read pinned comments | "App name is in my bio! 🔗" |
| **In-content CTA** | Show the app in use in the background WITHOUT mentioning it. People ask about it in comments | (Most powerful — creates curiosity) |
| **End screen** | Brief screenshot/text at the end of the video | App store screenshot or app name |

### The Golden Rule
**Don't overdo it.** Subtlety converts better than hard selling. Use 1-2 of these per video, not all 4. The in-content CTA (showing without telling) is the most powerful because it creates organic curiosity.

## Proven Formats

### Format 1: Couples Doodle (Lovelee-style)
Two phones side by side, one person doodles → appears on partner's screen in real time.

**Specs:** 10 seconds, vertical (9:16), trending romantic/cute audio

**Caption templates:**
- "pov: [action] for your [bf/gf/partner] 💕"
- "may this type of [emotion] find you 🥹"
- "when your partner [does sweet thing] using this app"
- "long distance couples need this app 🥺"

**Why it works:** Visual satisfaction + emotional trigger + low barrier to relate + "what app is this?" comments = free marketing. The LDR (long-distance relationship) angle is massively underserved.

### Format 2: AI Slideshows (Larry Method)
AI-generated images in slideshow format with text overlays and trending audio.

**Specs:** 5-15 seconds (3-6 slides), portrait orientation

**Structure:**
- Slide 1 = hook (bold text, surprising visual)
- Middle slides build curiosity or deliver value
- Final slide = CTA or reveal
- Text overlays added programmatically

**Key stat:** TikTok slideshows get 2.9x more comments, 1.9x more likes, 2.6x more shares vs video. Algorithm actively pushing photo content.

### Format 3: UGC-Style Discovery
Person shows app on screen with excited narration or text overlay. Feels like genuine discovery, not an ad.

**Example:** "Found the cutest app for LDR couples" — the "what app?" comments drive algorithm engagement.

### Format 4: Before/After Transformation
Show a problem → show the app solving it. Best for utility apps, design apps, photo editing.

### General Format Principles
1. **Hook in first 1 second** — text overlay or surprising visual
2. **10 seconds or less** — completion rate is everything for algorithm
3. **Trending audio** — ride what's hot
4. **Emotional triggers** — love, FOMO, curiosity, relatability
5. **Show don't tell** — the app in action, not talking about it

## Caption & Hashtag Strategy

### Captions
- Lead with the hook (first line is everything)
- Mention the app naturally — don't force it
- Emotional hooks outperform descriptive ones
- Write like a person, not a brand

### Hashtags
- **Max 4-5 hashtags** per post
- Mix **broad** (#couplegoals #relationship) with **niche** (#ldr #couplesapp)
- Include one app-specific hashtag if it feels natural
- Don't use banned or saturated hashtags

## Image Generation Tips (for AI Slideshow Format)

### The Critical Rule: Lock the Architecture
Write one incredibly detailed room/scene description and copy-paste into every prompt. Only change the STYLE between slides.

**Lock:** room dimensions, window count/position, door location, camera angle, furniture size, ceiling height, floor type.
**Change:** wall color, bedding, decor, lighting fixtures, style name.

### Prompt Best Practices
- Include "iPhone photo" and "realistic lighting" — makes images look like real phone photos
- "Before" rooms need to look modern but tired, not derelict
- Add signs of life: flat screen TV, mugs, remote control, everyday items
- Without everyday items, rooms look like empty show homes — nobody relates
- **Don't add people** — doesn't work with current image gen

### Orientation
- **Portrait:** 1024x1536 (NOT 1536x1024 landscape — causes black bars on TikTok)

---

## Text Overlay Rules

- **Font size:** 6.5% of canvas height minimum (5% is unreadable on mobile)
- **Position:** NOT too high — hidden behind TikTok's status bar
- **Line length:** Must fit max canvas width or rendering compresses text horizontally
- **Test on phone** before posting — desktop looks different

## Performance Tracking

### What to Log for Every Post
- Post ID, platform, caption, format type
- Views, likes, saves, comments (update at 24h and 7 days)
- Download/subscriber attribution if possible
- What hook formula was used
- Whether audio was trending

### Weekly Review Cadence
1. Review the week's posts — rank by views and engagement
2. Identify patterns: what hooks worked, what formats got saves
3. Double down on top performers — make variations
4. Kill what's not working — no sunk cost attachment
5. Brainstorm 10-15 hooks for next week, referencing performance data
6. Pre-generate content overnight using batch APIs (50% cheaper)
