# Copy Scoring Rubric

Quality gate for scoring copy. Used in Write mode (Phase 5) and Review mode (Phase R1). Score every piece of copy against these 7 dimensions before presenting to the user.

---

## Scoring Scale

Each dimension is scored **Red / Yellow / Green**:

- **Green**: Strong — meets or exceeds the standard, no action needed
- **Yellow**: Functional but has clear improvement opportunities
- **Red**: Failing — actively hurting the copy's effectiveness, must fix before shipping

---

## Dimension 1: Readability

Does the copy read easily? Can a smart 8th grader understand it?

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Grade level | 4-8 | 9-10 | 11+ |
| Passive voice | < 5% of sentences | 5-15% | > 15% |
| Avg sentence length | 12-18 words | 18-22 words | 22+ words |
| Adverb density | < 1 per 100 words | 1-3 per 100 words | 3+ per 100 words |
| Jargon | None, or explained on first use | Some unexplained | Jargon-heavy |
| Paragraph length | 1-4 sentences | 5-6 sentences | 7+ sentence blocks |

---

## Dimension 2: Slippery Slope

Does each sentence compel the reader to read the next? Can the reader stop mid-way?

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Paragraph transitions | Smooth bridges between every paragraph | Most transitions work, 1-2 gaps | Choppy, disconnected paragraphs |
| Open loops | 2-3 well-placed open loops that pay off | 1 open loop or loops that don't close | No open loops, or loops that frustrate |
| Exit ramps | No natural stopping points before the CTA | 1-2 minor exit ramps | Multiple places where the reader feels "done" |
| Curiosity gaps | Reader consistently wants to know what's next | Some curiosity but inconsistent | Flat — reader could take or leave the next paragraph |
| First 3 sentences | Irresistible — impossible not to keep reading | Solid but not magnetic | Weak, generic, or could be skipped |

### Exit Ramp Audit

Walk through the copy paragraph by paragraph. After each, ask: "Could the reader stop here and feel satisfied?" Mark every exit ramp and fix with:
1. An open loop that hasn't closed yet
2. A transition bridge ("But here's where it gets interesting...")
3. A tease of what's coming
4. A question that demands an answer

---

## Dimension 3: Emotional Resonance

Does the copy make the reader FEEL something? Copy that informs but doesn't move is content, not copy.

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Conflict/tension | Clear tension between problem and solution | Some tension but understated | No conflict — reads like a product manual |
| Transformation story | Vivid before/after that reader can see themselves in | Implied transformation but not shown | No transformation — just features |
| Empathy | Reader feels understood ("they get me") | Some understanding shown | Generic — could be written for anyone |
| Emotional driver | Clear primary emotion (frustration, aspiration, fear, curiosity) | Emotion present but unfocused | Emotionally flat |
| Specificity of pain | Describes the exact moment/feeling of the problem | Describes the problem category | Vague problem statement |

### Emotional Driver Check

Every piece of copy should tap into at least one primary emotion:
- **Frustration**: "You've tried everything and nothing works"
- **Aspiration**: "Imagine what you could do with..."
- **Fear of missing out**: "While you're still doing X, your competitors are..."
- **Curiosity**: "There's something most people don't know about..."
- **Relief**: "Finally, a way to stop worrying about..."
- **Pride**: "Join the top 1% who..."

---

## Dimension 4: Specificity

Vague copy is dead copy. Specific copy is believable, visual, and memorable.

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Claims | Specific, falsifiable claims with evidence | Mix of specific and vague | All vague ("the best," "world-class," "innovative") |
| Numbers | Concrete numbers used where possible | Some numbers, some vague | No numbers, all qualitative |
| Examples | Real, specific examples that illustrate points | Generic examples | No examples |
| Visuals | Reader can picture what you're describing | Somewhat visual | Abstract and conceptual |
| Uniqueness | Claims nobody else could make | Some unique claims | Could describe any competitor |

### Harry Dry's 3 Rules (Apply to Every Key Claim)

| Rule | Test | Example (Pass) | Example (Fail) |
|------|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Visualize** | Can you picture it? | "1,000 songs in your pocket" | "Innovative digital music solution" |
| **Falsify** | Can it be proven true/false? | "Saves 4 hours per week" | "Improves efficiency" |
| **Uniqueness** | Can nobody else say this? | "The only CRM built for solo founders" | "The best CRM for your business" |

Three fails on a key claim = mandatory rewrite.

---

## Dimension 5: Voice Authenticity

Does the copy sound like a real person wrote it? Would you read this aloud without cringing?

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Read-aloud test | Sounds natural spoken aloud | Mostly natural, 1-2 awkward spots | Sounds like a brochure or press release |
| Conversational markers | Uses contractions, fragments, direct address naturally | Some but inconsistent | Formal, stiff, third-person |
| Brand consistency | Voice matches brand personality (if Story System exists) | Mostly consistent | Mismatched or generic |
| AI detection | No AI cliches, no formulaic patterns | 1-2 AI-sounding phrases | Reads like ChatGPT wrote it |
| Personality | Has a clear point of view, takes stances | Some personality | Bland, could be written by anyone |

### AI Cliche Watchlist

Immediately rewrite if you catch any of these:
- "In today's [fast-paced/digital/competitive] [landscape/world/era]"
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Whether you're a [X] or a [Y]..."
- "Look no further"
- "Unlock the power of..."
- "Seamlessly integrate..."
- "Take your [X] to the next level"
- "Harness the potential..."
- "Navigate the complexities of..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge"

---

## Dimension 6: Copy Length

Is the copy the right length for its purpose, audience, and product?

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Every sentence earns its place | Cut any sentence and the copy suffers | Some sentences could be cut without loss | Significant padding and repetition |
| Objection coverage | All major objections addressed | Most objections addressed | Key objections unanswered |
| Desire is sufficient | Reader has enough desire to act by the end | Almost enough, could use more proof | Not enough — reader still skeptical |
| Length matches context | Appropriate for copy type + price + awareness | Slightly long or short | Way too long (bloated) or too short (rushed) |

### Quick Length Check

- **Too long signals**: Repeating the same point in different words, padding with unnecessary examples, addressing objections the audience doesn't have
- **Too short signals**: Jumping to the CTA before building desire, skipping objection handling, assuming familiarity the reader doesn't have

---

## Dimension 7: Framework Execution

Is the chosen framework implemented correctly? Does the structure serve the copy's goal?

| Criteria | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Framework choice | Right framework for copy type + audience | Acceptable choice but not optimal | Wrong framework entirely |
| Structure adherence | All framework stages present and properly ordered | Most stages present, 1 weak/missing | Framework is mentioned but not actually followed |
| Transitions between stages | Seamless — reader doesn't feel the structure | Adequate but noticeable seams | Jarring shifts between stages |
| CTA integration | CTA flows naturally from the framework's logic | CTA present but feels bolted on | No clear CTA or CTA disconnects from the narrative |

---

## Overall Verdict

After scoring all 7 dimensions:

| Verdict | Criteria | Action |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Ship** | All Green, or all Green with 1 Yellow | Present to user with confidence |
| **Revise** | 2+ Yellows, or 1 Red with the rest Green/Yellow | Fix the weak dimensions, re-score |
| **Rewrite** | 2+ Reds, or the copy fundamentally doesn't work | Start over with a different approach/framework |

---

## Review Mode Usage

In Review mode (Phase R1), score the user's existing copy against this full rubric. Present:

1. The scoring summary table
2. Detailed notes for any Red or Yellow dimensions
3. Specific passages that need attention (quote them)
4. What's working well (celebrate Green dimensions)
5. Prioritized fix list (Reds first, then Yellows by impact)
