It doesn’t get any better than this.
Today’s assignment is one of the most famous sales letters ever.
This advertisement ran continuously, with minor changes, for twenty eight years from 1975 to 2003. And this style of storytelling was used since the early 1900s.
This letter sold over $2 billion worth of subscriptions for the WSJ. That’s roughly $200,000 a day for over 25 years. Other than minor price edits, it ran mostly unchanged the entire time.
Just two pages long, it was the workhorse for the Wall Street Journal.
Here’s what you should learn:
1.  Storytelling is an amazing way to grab people’s attention and get them interested. Stories are the best way to create a slippery slope.
2.  Starting a sales letter with a story…and THEN selling…is a great way to sell. Storytelling can be the Attention and the Interest in AIDA.
3.  After storytelling, explain the facts of the offer (Desire) and tell the Action.
I’ve made millions using this format.
A few examples:
1.  I made ~$1.5m sending an email launching Trends: The Market Talks [2]​
2.  The first Trends.co [3] landing page had a story about Jeff Bezos: Petri dish [4]​
3.  I made millions in ticket sales for Hustle Con. Most our sales came from email marketing and all the emails started with stories: You can find all the early emails in this post [5].
4.  Even the landing page for THIS course has a story: CopyThat [6]​
After reading today’s assignment, you’ll likely recognize it throughout the Internet. So many people copy it because it’s just that good.
So, without further ado, for today’s assignment please write out the greatest sales copy every written.
