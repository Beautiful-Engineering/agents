The best sales copy is like a slippery slope that you slowly fall down. The words suck you right in.
This week you'll learn a few ways to do that.
The top thing I want you to learn in today’s lesson: **Make your writing sing.**
**Give it rhythm!**
Your first assignment is my favorite non-obvious example of sales copy.
In 2015 (before he was canceled) Louis CK self-released a comedy album.
His sales page was a simple blog post [2]. And it was a masterpiece in writing in a simple and engaging way in order to sell!
(This post has since been taken down. I found it doing some detective work. You're welcome.)
*   Dry yet intriguing.
*   He uses simple language.
*   The flow is beautiful. His writing sings!
*   He uses self-deprecation and honesty that sells
*   And his rhythm is poetic.
Am I gushing over this simple post too much? Not at all. You’ll see for yourself.
