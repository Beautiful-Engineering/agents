# Ad Testing Toolkit

## How to Use the Ad Testing Toolkit

The toolkit isn’t about copying templates, it’s about **solving real business problems with creative tests.** Use it to go from “we don’t know what to try” → “we have 3–5 smart ad tests running” in minutes.

Here's the link to [our toolkit](https://demandcurve.notion.site/Ad-Testing-Toolkit-23ff852211328021993fddd17e664335?source=copy_link) that you'll use while making ad creatives.

### **Step 1: Start With the Business Problem**

Before you touch creative, ask: *What’s actually holding back growth?*

Typical categories:

- **Awareness** → Not enough people seeing the brand.
- **Audience Alignment** → Attracting the wrong users.
- **Conversion** → People click but don’t sign up/buy.
- **Retention/Activation** → People try it but don’t stick.
- **Positioning/Monetization** → No clear differentiation, or price vs. value mismatch.

### **Step 2: Turn the Problem Into a Hypothesis**

Write a one-sentence “if/then” statement:
*If we do X, then Y will improve because Z.*

Example:

- Problem: People don’t believe our product works.
- Hypothesis: *If we combine hard stats with customer proof, then conversions will rise because prospects trust us more.*

### **Step 3: Choose Your Creative Building Blocks**

Use the toolkit menus to assemble an ad:

- **Pick a Hook Type** → How you’ll grab attention (Curiosity Gap, Problem-Agitate, Contrarian, Social Proof, etc.).
- **Pick an Ad Format** → How you’ll package it (Static Testimonial, This vs That, UGC Demo, Meme, etc.).
- **Pick a CTA** → How you’ll close (Urgency, Value-Driven, Social Proof, Curiosity, Action-Oriented).
This mix gives you dozens of combinations. You don’t need to overthink — just build 3–5 variations quickly.

### **Step 4: Run Structured Tests**

Don’t test randomly — test in layers:

- **Round 1 → Hook Test**
Try 3 hooks that address the same problem.
*Metric: CTR (does anyone care?)*
- **Round 2 → Format Test**
Take the winning hook, try it in 3 different ad formats.
*Metric: Conversion Rate (does the message work?)*
- **Round 3 → CTA Test**
Take the winning hook+format combo, try 3 CTAs.
*Metric: CPA (is it profitable?)*

### **Step 5: Capture Learnings**

For every test, note:

- What problem it was solving
- Which hook/format/CTA you used
- Which metric moved
Over time, you’ll build a **library of proven creative angles** specific to your product.
