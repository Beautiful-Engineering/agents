## Ad creative tool stack

Although researching and playing with tools is often a form of procrastination, for ad creatives in particular the right tool can make something 10x more efficient than using legacy software.

Here's a list of recommended tools.

## Design tools

Tools to make static images and ad creatives.

- [**Figma**](https://figma.com/) – Free
- Personally, I use this to make all of my designs, but I’m a web developer. For most people, I’d recommend Canva.

- [**Canva**](https://www.canva.com/) – Free to $10/mo
- Really easy-to-use design tool with a ton of built-in assets and templates.

- [**Remove.bg**](https://remove.bg/) – Free + premium
- Super easy-to-use tool that uses AI to crop out backgrounds from images. Way faster and more effective than Photoshop.

- [**Midjourney**](https://www.midjourney.com/) – $10/mo
- One of my favorite tools ever made. I make a lot of cool imagery with it.

- [**Iconscout**](https://iconscout.com/) – Free to $15/mo.
- Huge library of icons, illustrations, and animations.

## Video editing

- [Capcut](https://capcut.com/): Our go-to video editor, and it's free.
- [Final Cut Pro](https://www.apple.com/final-cut-pro/): A paid video editor from Apple.
- [iMovie](https://www.apple.com/imovie/): Apple's built-in video editor. Only use if you're familiar with it.
- [Descript](https://www.descript.com/): Awesome tool where you edit videos by editing text. Built more for podcasts.

## Creative ad agencies

Creating ads is hard. There are productized services that do this for you. You just need to tell them what to make and their designers will make them:

- [GetAds](https://getads.co/?via=dc): We use them internally at DC to augment our team. Quite affordable and great at static assets.
- [Superside](https://www.superside.com/ad-creative): A more premium option.
