**The day you became a better writer.**
​
​
[1]
​
“If you were starting a business today, what would it be?”
I loathe that question! It’s a stupid, no good, stinkin’ ask that people DM me daily on Twitter.
It’s a useless question because there are a hundred good and true answers.
Instead, what they should ask is “what skillset do you wish you acquired earlier in your career?”
My answer is easy: copywriting. More specifically, I wish I were told earlier: “write like you speak.”
Remember your 9th grade English class? “Be descriptive,” your teacher hammered into your head.
And so, you started using words like “evident” instead of “obvious.”
And sentences like “The tall muscular man took the beautiful girl’s slender hand and kissed it with his luscious full lips, until her wild beating heart fluttered with emotion.”
Oy vey!
Have you ever spoken the word “luscious” in your entire life?
And as you get older, this habit takes over your professional life. Just look at people’s bios on LinkedIn.
Procured. Utilized. Exceptional.
People do this because they think it makes them sound smart. But it doesn’t. This style of language commits the biggest sin of all: being basic!
So, today’s takeaway is this: **Write like you speak.**
Great copywriting, or really any writing for that matter, should use everyday language that sounds like it's coming straight from your mouth.
Your goal is to use the written word to get your ideas into your reader’s brain and get them to take the action you want them to take.
This means that proper grammar isn’t the most important part of your writing (a lot of people don’t realize this). And sounding smart and important with big words - that’s for losers.
Today’s example of this is one of my favorite copywriting techniques: **letter from the founder.**
Jason Fried founded a popular email business called HEY. On the sales homepage, he wrote a wonderful letter from his personal perspective on why HEY is different and worth the user’s money.
This is a wonderful sales technique that’s underused on a lot of websites and startups.
So, get to it...Hey.com [2] homepage letter!
