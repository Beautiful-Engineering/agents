Want to become a good writer? Then you have to start with good writing.

Imagine if on the first day of culinary school the instructor says “for your first assignment, please go and cook good food!”

How stupid would that be?

Instead, you spend the first part of your education mastering other people’s classic recipes. In doing this, you learn the patterns of great cooking. You understand the texture of greatness.

We learn music the same way. Copying other people’s great work.

But after a while you start to make your own creations. Stealing what you like from others and combining it together.

And then voilà...now you have your own style.

We can learn how to write in the exact same way.

This process is called copywork. Up until the 20th century this was the primary way people learned to write. Ben Franklin, Jack London, even the director Judd Apatow was a copywork fan.

But for some reason this method of learning has gone away.

I think that's stupid. Why? Because it's the best and quickest way to learn how to write.

So, this 10 day course will teach you how to write better sales copy. Sales emails, landing pages, whatever.

The goal is to teach you how to get people to do what you want them to do. Simple.

You'll get 1 email a day (only on weekdays).

Your mission:

Read and absorb. Read the email, which teaches a copywriting lesson. Then, read the attached PDF - this is the piece of writing you'll copy. Read my commentary in the margins. I'll point out what makes it great.
Copy the PDF by hand. Write it by hand on a piece of paper. Only spend 30 minutes doing it. No biggie if you haven’t completed the entire letter in 30 minutes.
Do not miss a day. This can potentially change your life. Skip one day and you’ll likely skip the rest. Don’t screw yourself. Same time every day.
Make a swipe file. Throughout the day, as you see writing you like, save it. A swipe file is just a folder of writing examples you like. Could be a document, Dropbox folder, Pinterest board, whatever.
Before writing anything important, spend 3-5 minutes copying your favorite writing. This will get you in the groove.
